# SQ-AXIS-03 — The Silent Wreath-Order

## Introduction

A small order tends the Axis amphitheater's wreath-stone in total, ritual silence,
genuinely believing that their unbroken maintenance is what holds the world together — a
missed rite, in their creed, would end everything. It never was so; but this quest does
*not* tell the player, through the order or otherwise, what actually holds the world. It
only follows the order's oldest member as she finally admits — to the Fool alone, the one
outsider safe to tell — that their tending was never load-bearing, and lays down an office
she never needed to carry. The relief is the story. The truth beyond it stays where
narrative.md keeps it.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** The Wreath-Order at their work: robed figures tending the white wreath-
   stone with unbroken, precise gestures, silent for three hundred years. A newcomer is
   expected to keep the silence and watch.
2. **The creed.** Through gesture, a written rule shown to the Fool, or one member willing
   to breach the silence, the Fool learns the order's belief: their maintenance keeps the
   world whole, and a single missed rite would unmake it. (Theme 2: an office that has
   eaten its people — the rite as a role calcified over centuries into the whole shape of
   their lives.)
3. **The doubt.** Elder Sister Loveday of the Wreath-Order (characters.md §Regional named
   NPCs), the oldest among them, has quietly stopped believing it. She draws the Fool
   aside — precisely *because* the Fool belongs to no order and can be told safely.
4. **Her admission.** Their tending was never what held anything. The world stood, and
   goes on standing, with or without their sweeping of the stone. She says it with relief,
   not despair — the particular ease of a person setting down a weight she now sees she
   was never really carrying. **Crucially, Loveday does not claim to know what *does* hold
   the world; she knows only that it was not them.** She never names the Dancer's role —
   she cannot know it, and the quest must not supply it.
5. **The Fool's part.** The Fool witnesses, and may answer. `[Fool choice, ≤ 12 words:
   earnest — "Maybe the tending was for you, not the world." / foolish — "Fine stone. Worth
   the polish either way."]` The Fool neither confirms nor denies the order's cosmology —
   the Fool has no standing to, and reticence forbids more.
6. **The ache and the laugh.** The honest ache: Loveday grieves the decades of silence
   spent on an unneeded duty. The laugh: she notes, dry as an old bell, that at least the
   stone is *very* clean — and breaks three hundred years of vow purely to complain about
   the amphitheater's acoustics.
7. **`[If CONFESSED]`.** The Fool, by now, knows far more than Loveday ever will about what
   holds the world — and still says nothing. Her relief is real, and the Fool lets it be:
   tender withholding, played in the quiet. No option is added that reveals the Dancer or
   the twist.

## Open questions

- Should breaking the order's silence grant any Renown at all? (Recommend minimal-to-none:
  the payoff is the witnessing, and the Axis has no home suit to bank Renown with.)
- The dialogue for both Loveday and the Fool needs a human-level authoring pass at script
  status to guarantee neither ever asserts what holds the world together — the quest's
  reticence around the Dancer is its single load-bearing constraint.
- Whether the order's belief is conveyed by breached silence or entirely by gesture and
  written rule — a tone decision affecting how much dialogue the silent order can carry.
