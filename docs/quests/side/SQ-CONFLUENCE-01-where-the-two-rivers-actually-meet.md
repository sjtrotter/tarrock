# SQ-CONFLUENCE-01 — Where the Two Rivers Actually Meet

## Introduction

A surveyor's puzzle homed at the Confluence and built to reward the player who reads the
land as land. The delta's mixers have argued for three centuries over which of the two
rivers is "senior" at the eternal pour, and treat the question as high philosophy with
schools, factions, and inherited doctrine. One apprentice is tired of being told the
answer is unknowable, and the Fool helps her simply go and measure it. The quest leans
directly on the world's `Hydrology rule`: the rivers meet exactly where gravity says a
confluence must, no matter what any doctrine claims — and the "eternal pour" turns out to
be plumbing, paused, not metaphysics. Taking up the Mixer's-apprentice Calling
(`callings.md`) is the natural way in.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Delphine Marchetti (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), a mixer's
   apprentice, is sick of the seniority debate — which river yields to which at the pour,
   argued as if the answer governed the tides. Her own elders hold rival positions and
   have for generations. She wants to *measure*, not debate, and needs a second pair of
   hands and someone with a dog who can climb.
2. **The survey.** The Fool and Delphine trace each river up its visible course. Per the
   `Hydrology rule`, every reach runs downhill from a credible source toward the sea;
   there is nothing level, nothing forking uphill, nothing arriving from behind a hill.
   The land is honest even where the doctrine isn't.
3. **The wet climb.** Reaching the true meeting point means a soaked scramble behind the
   great cups, to the lower channel the pour has hidden for 300 years. There, plainly,
   the two rivers join exactly where physics requires — the "senior river" question
   dissolves the instant anyone actually looks.
4. **The reveal.** The eternal pour is simply water that has been waiting three centuries
   to finish arriving at the sea. It was never philosophy. It was the Stall pausing a
   perfectly ordinary confluence mid-arrival.
5. **The honest beat.** Delphine is thrilled and then, quietly, a little bereft — the
   mystery she grew up inside was only water, and the elders who argued it so beautifully
   were her family. She resolves to publish not to humiliate them but because the plain
   truth is kinder than the endless argument. The Fool can be earnest or dry — "Let's go
   and look." / "Or we could ask the water." (≤12 words, one earnest option).
6. **The reaction (the laugh).** The mixers' guild is scandalized, then delighted; one
   ancient doctrinaire admits he stopped believing his own school decades ago and simply
   loved the arguing, and asks, wounded, whatever they're all meant to talk about now.

**[If WS_TEMPERANCE_UNBOUND: …]** — If Temperance is already unbound, the delta has
drained and the rivers now visibly reach the sea. Delphine's survey is vindicated in the
most literal way possible: her predicted meeting-line is exactly where the water now runs
across the new grassland, and her measurement becomes the first accurate map of the drained
delta. The old seniority doctrine is retired without ceremony — you cannot argue with a
river you can walk up to.
