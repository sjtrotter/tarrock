# SQ-MAW-02 — The Trophy Hunter's Empty Wall

## Introduction

Wren Astley built her whole self out of being the Maw's finest hunter — a lodge with
trophies floor to ceiling, a wall of proof that she is the best there has ever been at
one hard thing. Then Strength was unbound, the wild went neutral-until-provoked, and the
one hard thing stopped meaning what it meant. The player meets a woman standing in front
of her own wall, deciding whether she was ever anything besides it. This quest does not
let the Fool answer that for her. In keeping with the Maw's dry, hard-won grace, it stays
honestly unresolved — the player's job is to be present while a proud person looks at the
end of their whole reason and decides, alone, what comes after.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Post-`WS_STRENGTH_UNBOUND`, Wren Astley (`characters.md` §Regional named
   NPCs — promoted in the parallel slate change) sits in her trophy lodge with her spear
   across her knees and nothing left to point it at. Beasts no longer charge; a hunt that
   isn't a fight isn't the hunt she was famous for. She is not grieving the animals. She
   is grieving what she was *for*.
2. **The complication.** Wren doesn't want comfort and will bark down pity fast. What she
   asks, testing the Fool, is a straight hunter's question with no clean answer: is a
   skill still a skill when the thing it was for is gone? The wall behind her answers one
   way; the quiet Maw outside answers another.
   - Fool (earnest): "The tracking was always yours." / (foolish): "Hunt me. I'm easy."
3. **The last outing.** Wren proposes one more trip into the crags — not to kill, she's
   careful to say, just to see if she still reads the ground. The Fool accompanies her on
   a rescue-flavored track (a bogged, unharmed beast, per the Maw's changed hunts) where
   every instinct she has still works perfectly and none of it ends in a trophy.
4. **The ache.** She frees the beast cleanly, expertly, and it walks off without so much
   as looking back — the exact competence that once made a kill now making a mercy, and
   she cannot decide whether that is a promotion or a demotion of everything she is. She
   does not pretend to the Fool that it settles anything.
5. **The laugh.** Back at the lodge she offers to teach the Fool to skin a trophy, catches
   herself, and instead teaches them to read a hoofprint — grumbling that it's a poor
   trade for a good skinning knife, and that the knife will "rust honest now, at least."
6. **Closing beat.** Wren does not take down her wall and does not add to it; whether she
   becomes a tracker-for-rescue, a teacher, or simply a woman with a spear she no longer
   raises is left to her, unresolved on purpose. Her barks shift from boasts to
   questions. The Fool leaves without the answer, which is the quest's whole point.
