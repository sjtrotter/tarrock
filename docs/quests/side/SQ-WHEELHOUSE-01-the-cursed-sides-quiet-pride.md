# SQ-WHEELHOUSE-01 — The Cursed Side's Quiet Pride

## Introduction

The Wheel has turned. Luck cycles now, on a schedule, and for the first time in three
hundred years the Wheelhouse's eternally-cursed district can catch a good day. That
sounds like an unqualified joy, and it isn't — because a whole community grew up inside
the shared bad luck, held together by gallows humor and mutual aid, and real fortune is
the one thing that solidarity was never built to survive. There is no boss and no dungeon
here: only a neighborhood deciding, out loud, what kind of neighbors they want to be now
that the wheel remembers how to spin. The Fool arrives with no stake and no luck of the
local kind, which is exactly why they're allowed into the room where it's argued.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** In the once-cursed district, Della Quill (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), the elder who has kept
   the district's spirit for three centuries, tends the "Bad Luck Book" — a communal
   ledger of everyone's disasters, read aloud on the worst evenings so the whole street
   can laugh at misfortune together. She's proud of what shared calamity built: shared
   pantries, shared roofs, nobody ever cursed alone.
2. **The complication.** Since `WS_FORTUNE_UNBOUND`, one resident's luck has genuinely
   turned — winnings, a mended leg, a dry roof for the first time in living memory. Not
   from malice but from arithmetic, the mutual-aid economy assumed no one would ever have
   more than anyone else, and now someone does. The lucky neighbor is quietly, miserably
   set apart; the district's whole grammar has no word for good fortune except *cheating*.
3. **Gathering the voices.** Through small errands and doorway conversations the Fool
   collects the district's positions: pool the winnings and keep the old ways; let people
   keep their own luck and let the aid-web loosen; or split the difference and quietly
   resent both. No position is villainous; every one costs something real.
4. **The engineered moment.** A district meeting at the mutual-aid hall. The Fool can
   argue for pooling, argue for individual keeping, or simply keep the meeting from
   collapsing into a shouting match long enough for the neighbors to hear each other.
   Whichever, the decision is theirs — the Fool brokers, never rules.
5. **The ache and the laugh.** Della admits the hardest part was never the luck. Being
   cursed together was the one thing that made them *need* each other, and she is grieving
   a purpose the way Clerk Pettibone grieves a queue — the office of "the elder who holds
   the cursed street together" is dissolving under her. The laugh: she resolves to keep
   the Bad Luck Book going anyway, now recording good luck too, "so we've something to be
   smug about of an evening." The ache stays: some neighbors will drift once no one's roof
   depends on the next roof's kindness.
6. **Closing beat.** The district's greeting barks and its aid-rituals persist, soften,
   or thin out per the meeting's outcome. Della keeps her Book. No `WS_*` flag is set, and
   no other quest reads this result.
