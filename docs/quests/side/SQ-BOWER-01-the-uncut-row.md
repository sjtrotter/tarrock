# SQ-BOWER-01 — The Uncut Row

## Introduction

Out in the Bower's endless, uncuttable wheat, a farmhand named Tibb Wren has a theory: if
he can just finish *one* single row before it regrows behind him, the whole curse will
break. He is wrong. He knows he is wrong. And he does it every single morning anyway,
timing his scythe-strokes like a man racing a tide he has never once beaten and never
will. The player can lend a hand for a run at it, and the helping is earnest and useless
in exactly equal measure — which is rather the entire point. It is the Bower's central
futility made into a person you can stand beside and swing alongside. If the Empress is
unbound while Tibb is mid-row, the ending waits for him in a shape he chooses himself.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** At the ragged edge of a wheat-field with no far side, Tibb Wren (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) is scything at a
   pace that would kill a younger man, glancing over his shoulder between every stroke.
   He explains, cheerfully and without hope, his theory: finish one row before it grows
   back and the Bower's whole stasis snaps. He offers the Fool a spare billhook.
2. **The attempt.** The Fool works the row alongside him — a rhythm-of-work stretch that
   is the Farmhand Calling's futility rendered as a set-piece: cut, and watch the stalks
   behind you stand back up green, the finish line receding exactly as fast as you close
   on it (per `callings.md`, pre-MQ03 nothing may be *finished* being cut). They will not
   reach the end. The game lets the player *feel* that before anyone says it.
3. **The complication.** Between runs, Tibb admits the thing that makes him more than a
   joke: he stopped believing in the theory decades ago. He keeps at it because a man
   racing a tide at least has something to *do* with the morning, and the alternative — 
   standing still in a garden that will not let anything be finished — is worse. The
   earnest uselessness is a choice, not a delusion (theme 1).
4. **The ache and the laugh.** The Fool can throw everything into one more genuine
   attempt, coach Tibb to pace himself so he lasts the season, or simply keep him company
   through a run. The laugh: Tibb has strong, unrepeatable opinions about the *correct*
   technique for a race he has lost several thousand times. The honest beat: he thanks the
   Fool for being the first person in three hundred years to lose it *with* him.
5. **[If WS_EMPRESS_UNBOUND] The monument.** When the Empress is unbound and every harvest
   across the Spread completes at last, the wheat behind Tibb finally stays cut — except
   for the exact half-row he was working the moment it happened, which, by his own request,
   he leaves standing forever. Not a failure left as a scar; a monument to the mornings.
   He asks the Fool to help him rope it off, and grins the whole time.
