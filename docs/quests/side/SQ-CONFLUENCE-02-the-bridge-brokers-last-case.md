# SQ-CONFLUENCE-02 — The Bridge-Broker's Last Case

## Introduction

A post-unbinding comedy with an ache under it, available once the Fool has drained the
delta (`WS_TEMPERANCE_UNBOUND`). Osgood Fenn built a genuinely respected career mediating
the endless dispute over the Confluence's half-finished bridge — a role that required real
tact, precisely because the bridge could never, structurally, be finished. Then physics
finished it in an afternoon, and Osgood's entire profession, along with a rather large
sense of his own importance, evaporated with the receding water. The quest lets the Fool
watch a self-important man discover he was an office all along, and — gently — find the
smaller, truer, *endable* work that was hiding underneath it. Distinct from MQ14's Elgin
Thatch, who mourns the *stone*; Osgood mourns the *dispute*.

## Beats

1. **The finished bridge.** With the delta drained, the famous half-built span simply
   stands complete — the two banks joined by dry stone, scaffolding three centuries
   weathered and suddenly pointless. Travelers walk across without a second thought.
2. **The redundant broker.** Osgood Fenn (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), the
   celebrated bridge-broker, is out of a career overnight. His standing, his fees, his
   whole considerable self-regard rested on the bridge remaining an unsolvable problem —
   and it has been solved by weather.
3. **Denial (the comedy).** Osgood refuses the obvious. He convenes a mediation summit no
   one attends, drafts fresh terms for a bridge already open, and interviews baffled
   travelers mid-crossing about a dispute they have never heard of. The Fool can play
   straight man or gently press the truth.
4. **The honest beat.** Under the pomposity, Osgood is terrified of being unnecessary — a
   whole life's dignity built on a problem that was never meant to be solved, now solved
   without him and without thanks. The office ate the man; its ending has handed the man
   back, smaller than he'd like. The Fool's lines stay short and kind — "The case is
   closed, Osgood." / "You mediated it beautifully for years." (≤12 words, one earnest
   option).
5. **The real case.** There is one genuine mediation left in it: two riverside families
   quarrelling over who pays for the new span's now-necessary handrail, and right-of-way
   for their carts across it. Petty, real, and — crucially — *finishable*. Osgood takes it
   with enormous, badly-hidden relief, pretending all the while it is beneath him.
6. **The laugh.** He bills the two families extravagantly for settling a handrail, and
   they pay without a murmur, because some things about the Confluence's love of an
   elaborate transaction will never drain away.
7. **Closing beat.** Restored and diminished, Osgood becomes what he can actually be now:
   a mediator of small, real, endable disputes. Theme 2 & 3, plainly — the ending that
   took his office gave him back a life the right size.
