# SQ-MIRRORMARSH-02 — The One Who Didn't Want to Go Home

## Introduction

This quest opens only after the Moon is unbound (`WS_MOON_UNBOUND`), when the Mirrormarsh's
fog lifts and its "monsters" are revealed as lost people — a region-wide happy ending the
quest exists to complicate honestly. A family that has searched the fog for a missing
relative for generations, braced for either a reunion or a grave, gets neither: the Fool
finds him alive, well, and wholly unwilling to be rescued. Inside the marsh's fog he built
an entire new life and a self he prefers, and the rescue everyone else calls a happy ending
is, to him, the loss of everything he made. The Fool carries his answer back — not the one
the family hoped for — and the region's newfound joy is given its one honest, unresolved
shadow. Theme 3, exactly.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** With the fog gone, a family (unnamed walk-ons) arrives to claim their
   long-lost relative at last — certain, after all this time, that they are coming to grieve
   a body or to save a broken man. They ask the Fool, who knows the cleared marsh, to help
   find him.
2. **The finding.** The Fool finds Corin Vesk (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) — not broken, not lost, not a monster. In the years the fog hid him
   he built a whole life out here: a self stripped of the name and the debts and the family
   role that had been suffocating him, and he has been, by his own lights, happier than he
   ever was "at home."
3. **The refusal.** Corin does not want the rescue. What his family mourns as a tragedy he
   experienced as an escape, and the "happy ending" of coming home would undo the only person
   he has ever wanted to be. He is not cruel about it and not deluded; he simply knows his own
   mind, and it is not the mind they are searching for.
4. **The go-between.** The Fool carries messages across the gap — and, in the end, delivers
   Corin's actual answer rather than a softened one or the family's hoped-for one. There is no
   route here that gives everyone what they want; the quest will not manufacture one.
5. **The honest beat / the laugh.** The family cannot understand it — how do you grieve
   someone who's standing right there and doing fine? The Fool cannot fix that, and isn't asked
   to. Corin gets the quest's one dry laugh: the fog, he says, was better company than his aunts
   ever were, and he'll take a lying marsh over a truthful family reunion any day. It's a joke.
   It's also completely sincere, which is what makes it land.
6. **Closing beat.** The Mirrormarsh's region-wide joy stands, and so does this: one freed man
   who calls his freedom a home the world calls a wound, and a family that must go back the way
   they came, carrying an answer instead of a son. Nothing is resolved. It is only, at last, honest.

## Open questions

- Corin Vesk sits close to MQ18's own mourner **Ivy Ashby** (a freed fog-person grieving the
  untethered self she was inside the fog) and to the Dim's slate premise "The One Who Stayed"
  (Ansel Corvenna, the "lost" son who built a second life). The three are distinct in intent —
  Ivy isn't ready to be herself, Corin actively refuses the family, Ansel was never truly lost —
  but they share a silhouette. Flagged so the Mirrormarsh doesn't run two "freed person who
  won't come back" beats (Ivy in MQ18, Corin here) too close together; recommend differentiating
  sharply at script status or spacing them.
- Whether the family should be promotable (a named household with its own barks) or stay
  unnamed. Recommend unnamed, to keep the slate's proposed roster to Corin alone.
