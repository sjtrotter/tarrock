# SQ-PRESTIGE-01 — The Vanishing Act

## Introduction

Somewhere between the main tent's roar and the quieter edges of the midway sits a smaller
attraction: the House of Mirrors, and its sideshow star turn, the Vanishing Box. Flick
will point the Fool toward it with his usual salesman's warmth ("best trick in the
Prestige that isn't the one trying to kill you, friend") and a quieter aside that the
stagehand who runs it hasn't smiled backstage in longer than anyone can say. The player
finds Bram mid-routine, patter worn thin as old velvet, performing a vanish that never
quite finishes. What the Fool uncovers backstage — with Pip's nose for hidden things —
is a three-hundred-year-old goodbye that got stuck the very night the whole world did. This quest may
be played before or after MQ01 ("The Greatest Trick"); if the Magician is already unbound,
the Prestige is packing up around this scene, and the mirror-maze Wyn has hidden in for
so long is quite literally being carried away in crates. Both timings are written below.

## QUEST: THE BROTHER IN THE GLASS

### INT. THE PRESTIGE: House of Mirrors, Front of House — NIGHT (perpetual showtime)

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The Fool follows Flick's tip past the ring-toss stalls to a canvas archway painted with
chipped stars. Inside, a modest crowd sits frozen mid-gasp around a tall mirrored
cabinet. A weathered stagehand — BRAM — stands beside it, mid-patter, gesturing for a
volunteer who will never be picked because the moment never advances.

[If WS_MAGICIAN_UNBOUND: The crowd has risen and gone. The archway canvas is half down,
crates stacked where the seats were. Bram is still beside the cabinet, alone, folding a
mirrored panel into straw packing with hands that keep stopping.]

Approaches Bram between routines. Pip sniffs at the cabinet's base and whines, once, at
a seam in the mirrored wood.

**Bram Random Lines** *(repeatable greeting; pick one at random)*

> "Step right up — or don't. She's not going anywhere. More's the pity."

> "Same box, same trick, same fellow who never comes back out. Some encore, eh?"

> "You've a dog with a nose for gaps, friend. Mine hasn't found one in three hundred years."

[If WS_MAGICIAN_UNBOUND: replace with —]

> "Packing her up at last. Funny — I always thought I'd be glad of that."

> "Careful of the glass. It's the only thing round here that still remembers anything."

> "Show's over, they tell me. Wish someone had told the box that three hundred years ago."

TRANSITION TO CUT SCENE

### CUT SCENE

**BRAM**
> My brother Wyn built half this cabinet with me. Best hands in the Prestige.

**BRAM**
> One night he says he'll do the vanish himself. Crowd loved him for it.

**BRAM**
> Mirror shut. Never opened. I've run this same trick since, waiting for the reveal.

**THE FOOL**
> How long ago was that?

**BRAM**
> Three hundred years, near enough. The very night the whole world learned to stand
> still with me.

**BRAM**
> Everyone says the trick went wrong. Easier than saying I lost my brother.

END CUT SCENE

### CHOICE DIALOG — Ask about Wyn *(all questions may be exhausted)*

| The Fool | Bram's response |
|---|---|
| What was Wyn like? | "Quick hands, quicker tongue. Hated staying anywhere past its welcome." |
| Did you ever look for him? | "Every night, from right here. Never once thought to look *behind* the mirrors." |
| I'll go and look, if you'll let me. | "Bless you for the offer. I've long since stopped hoping. Go on, then." |

**If the Fool asked "Did you ever look for him?":**

| The Fool | Bram's response |
|---|---|
| What's stopping you now? | "Three hundred years of an answer I already fear. Takes a stranger's nerve, that does." |

[All versions pick up here:]

Bram unlatches the cabinet's rear panel, hands shaking slightly, and steps aside.

**BRAM**
> Mind the glass. And mind yourself — some doors are shut for a reason.

### INT. THE PRESTIGE: House of Mirrors — Backstage Maze — CONTINUOUS

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

Behind the cabinet, the House of Mirrors reveals its works: a cramped switchback corridor
walled in salvaged glass, some warped, some spider-cracked, each one catching the Fool's
reflection a half-second late. Sends Pip ahead with Seek near a stack of crated panels;
he noses along the seams instead of ahead, tail low.

[If WS_MAGICIAN_UNBOUND: Half the maze is already crated for transport, the mirrors
stacked in straw. What's left standing is a single narrow run of glass the movers haven't
reached yet — and everything in it is running out of places to be.]

Following Pip's Seek finds a section of backing board pried loose long ago and fitted
back crookedly. Behind it: a narrow nook barely large enough for a person — a bedroll
gone thin as paper, a stub of candle, a tin cup, and a plank scored with tally marks in
long, patient rows, hundreds deep.

**QUERENT (V.O.)**
> Funny thing about mirrors, Fool. They only ever show you the one who's looking.

A second Seek pass, closer to the tally-marked plank, turns up a folded, sealed letter
wedged into a gap behind a loose mirror shard — soft with age, never opened, addressed in
a cramped hand: *Bram*.

### BARKS — House of Mirrors backstage

**Stagehand Random Lines**
> "Bram won't let anyone else touch that cabinet's insides. Not once, three hundred years."

> "Half of us think the trick's cursed. The other half just miss the applause."

**Tansy Quill (Troupe) Random Lines**
> "I built every illusion in this carnival, and that box is the one I never designed."

> "Something in there's always felt occupied, if you want my honest opinion of it."

**Sideshow-Queue Minor Random Lines**
> "Best act on the midway, and it never finishes. By the Deal, that's a proper tease."

[The next scene depends on whether the player finds Wyn's letter:]

**If the player finds the letter:**

The mirror behind the nook doesn't reflect the corridor. It shows a thin, grey-bearded
man sitting very still, listening. He flinches as the Fool's light finds him.

### CUT SCENE

**WYN**
> Well. That took someone three hundred years and a very good dog.

**WYN**
> I'm not a ghost. I checked. Regularly. It gets tedious, checking.

**THE FOOL**
> Bram thinks you're dead. Why hide from him?

**WYN**
> The vanish was meant to be my exit — off with the next town's caravan.

**WYN**
> Then the world stopped turning, and there was no next town to be off to.

**WYN**
> A month of hiding, I could've explained. Three hundred years of it, I couldn't.

[If WS_MAGICIAN_UNBOUND: —]

**WYN**
> They're packing my whole hiding place into crates. I've nowhere left to listen from.

### CHOICE DIALOG — Ask Wyn *(all questions may be exhausted)*

| The Fool | Wyn's response |
|---|---|
| Why didn't you just leave? | "Leave to where? The whole Spread was mid-trick too, by then." |
| Do you still love your brother? | "Every night. That's rather the whole problem, isn't it." |
| You should tell him yourself. | "Should. Yes. 'Should' has done a great deal of hiding for me." |

[All versions pick up here:] Wyn presses the unopened letter back toward the Fool's hand.

### CHOICE OPTIONS — What becomes of the letter *(first pick commits; confirm prompt)*

| Choice | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Give the letter to Bram | Bram reads it that night, alone, and weeps — then keeps performing the same vanish, but now leaves a second stool by the cabinet "for whoever's listening." Barks shift to hopeful waiting rather than grief. |
| Return it to Wyn, unread | Wyn keeps his secret and his hiding place; thanks the Fool quietly and presses a carved wooden top into their hand — "Wyn's, mind, not mine to give twice." Bram never learns more than what the Fool chooses to say aloud. |
| Press Wyn to deliver it himself | Wyn goes still a long moment, then steps past the Fool toward the front of house. Epilogue bark: Bram, mid-patter, sees a familiar face in the audience's mirror and drops every prop he's holding. |

[If WS_MAGICIAN_UNBOUND: whichever choice is made, Wyn must leave the nook that same
night regardless — the crates are loaded by morning. "Return it unread" becomes Wyn
vanishing into the packing-up carnival itself rather than staying hidden; "deliver it
himself" plays the same reunion, only rushed, luggage already half-loaded around them.]

**If the player does not find the letter:**

The nook is empty of any letter, but plainly lived-in — the tally marks alone are proof
enough of long, quiet years. Pushing further into the mirrors draws a voice, unseen:

**WYN (O.S.)**
> Please. Whatever you think you've found back here — leave it be. Leave *me* be.

The voice does not return, however the Fool answers. Wyn stays hidden; nothing further
can be found behind that glass tonight.

[If WS_MAGICIAN_UNBOUND: the voice is thinner, hurried. "They're taking the mirrors,
friend. I don't know where a man hides once his hiding place is in a crate."]

Returning to Bram with only the tally-marked plank as proof still lands.

**BRAM**
> Someone's been living back there? Three hundred years, and someone's been *living*
> back there?

**BRAM**
> Then he's not gone. Not gone. I can hold onto that. I can.

Bram's future greeting lines soften from grief toward a stubborn, unresolved hope; he
leaves the cabinet door unlatched every night from here on, just in case.
