# SQ-COINS-01 — The Honest Scale

## Introduction

Homed in the Noonlands' plains but built to define Coins culture wherever it recurs,
this quest is a trade dispute rather than a treasure hunt — measured, transactional,
dry-humored, exactly as Coins culture is written. Two grain merchants accuse each other
of running a rigged scale at the harvest market; the Fool's investigation turns up
something smaller and more interesting than fraud, and the quest's real point is that a
truly fair deal is, to this suit-culture, its own quiet form of care.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Merchants Grissom Tuppen and Marchpane Fell (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) have accused each other of weighting the
   market's shared grain-scale in their own favor, a dispute treated by both with the
   grave seriousness Coins culture reserves for fairness itself — no shouting, just cold,
   precise, increasingly personal accounting.
2. **The complication.** Neither is cheating. The scale itself has drifted, imperceptibly,
   over 300 years of stasis — a hairline warp in wood that's never once been re-seasoned
   because nothing in the Noonlands has been allowed to age naturally. Both merchants have
   been unconsciously "correcting" for a fault they never identified, which is why their
   figures never quite agree.
3. **The investigation.** The Fool can borrow calibration weights from a Confluence
   mixer or Bastion clerk (small cross-region errand, played briskly), examine the scale's
   construction directly (a short physical puzzle rewarding attention to detail, in the
   same register as the hydrology and craft puzzles elsewhere in the slate), or simply
   sit both merchants down and have them re-weigh the same sack together, watching the
   discrepancy appear in real time.
4. **The ache and the laugh.** Once the fault is found, neither merchant apologizes,
   exactly — Coins culture doesn't do effusive contrition — but each quietly adjusts
   their own past ledgers to refund the tiny discrepancies they'd been unknowingly taking,
   without being asked, which is the culture's actual language for "I'm sorry." The
   laugh: they immediately start a new, deeply serious argument over whose fault the
   *wood* was. The ache: underneath the dryness, both had genuinely started to believe
   the other was capable of cheating them — and are visibly relieved to be wrong.
5. **Closing beat.** The recalibrated scale becomes a small point of local pride; both
   merchants' future greeting barks include a dry, running joke about "honest weights,"
   which becomes shorthand across the market for trustworthiness generally.
