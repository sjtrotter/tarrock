# SQ-ASSIZE-02 — The Scribe Who Copied Nothing New

## Introduction

The Fool needs one particular old record out of the Assize's archive, and is sent to the
scribe who keeps it — and discovers that fetching a document here means spending an
afternoon watching a master work. Wilhelmina Coates has spent three centuries transcribing
the same dead-end filings with flawless, loving precision, taking genuine, unbothered pride
in penmanship nobody will ever read. This is the Assize's driest, gentlest comedy: a quest
with no antagonist, no puzzle worth the name, and a real ache underneath it — a life given
wholly to copying things that never mattered, chosen on purpose, and quietly content. It
plays while the courts still stand adjourned; the craft is the whole reward.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** The Fool wants a specific record — a name, a deed, an old family filing
   (kept self-contained; whatever errand brought them here). Coates (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) has it, but it is mid-copy,
   and she will not release an unfinished copy while the original cannot leave the archive.
   The Fool must wait — or help.
2. **The watch.** Coates works. She is not deluded and not tragic: she knows every filing
   she copies is an adjourned case no one will open, and she loves the *doing* — the nib,
   the line, the clean page — the way a Wands crafter loves a joint that fits. Dry comedy
   in her total, serene indifference to the point of it all.
3. **Lending a hand.** The Fool can help — rule lines, mix ink, keep the Queue-warden's tea
   coming (Calling hook) — or simply sit and let her talk. Either way her contentment is
   the quest, and it is oddly steadying, like Pip's.
4. **The ache and the laugh.** Theme 1, worn light: her life's work is perfect copies of
   things that never decided anything, in a court that never ended. The laugh: she confides
   that two hundred years ago she permitted herself a single flourish — one tiny curl on
   one capital — and has felt deliciously, privately guilty ever since; she finds the exact
   page to show the Fool. The honest beat: she says the copying is how she has kept time —
   every finished page a day she chose to spend well, and there have been a great many good
   days.
5. **Resolution.** She completes the record, hands it over with a blessing on good
   penmanship, and the Fool leaves with what they came for and something quieter besides —
   proof that even in the most stopped place, a person can choose their hours.
