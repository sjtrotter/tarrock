# SQ-UNDERVAULT-03 — The Vault-Teller's Ledger

## Introduction

A small, unhurried character study built around the most comfortable Calling in the game
(Vault-teller, `callings.md`). Prosper Vane weighs and ledgers deposits in the Undervault
with the serene contentment of a man who has never once questioned his cage — and one
offhand, perfectly cheerful line about it is the whole region's darkest comedy in
miniature, delivered without a flicker of self-pity. There is nothing to rescue here and
no one who wants rescuing. The quest simply lets the Fool be the one person who noticed,
and then watches Prosper go right back to weighing, entirely content. Comfort as horror,
worn completely lightly.

## Beats

1. **The workplace.** Whether the Fool takes up the Vault-teller Calling or simply calls
   at his window, Prosper Vane (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) is found weighing
   and ledgering deposits with genuine, unforced serenity — a Coins-culture man, ledger at
   his belt, at ease in the softest job below or above ground.
2. **The line.** Somewhere in the small talk, Prosper mentions — cheerfully, in passing —
   that he hasn't seen actual sunlight in longer than he can remember, and doesn't miss
   it. No self-pity, no bravado, no awareness that anything is wrong. The whole
   Undervault's horror in one contented sentence.
3. **The probing.** The Fool can push — ask about the surface, his family, whether he ever
   wants to leave — and every warm, reasonable answer makes it worse: Prosper isn't
   trapped, he's *happy*. This is the Devil's argument made cozy, and it does not need the
   Devil present to land. The Fool's lines stay short — "Do you ever miss the sun?" /
   "Your handwriting is lovely, at least." (≤12 words, one earnest option).
4. **The honest beat.** The ledger itself is the ache: Prosper's life's work records
   deposits that will never be withdrawn, because in the Undervault nothing ends and
   nothing leaves. His immaculate, beloved book is a record of things that will never
   move — and he is proud of it, rightly, and that pride is the saddest thing in it.
   Theme 1 inverted: a man who has never been offered an ending and cannot miss what he
   has never known.
5. **The only move.** The Fool cannot free him — cannot, and Prosper does not want it. The
   single real thing on offer is to be the one person who asked, and *meant* it. Prosper
   is quietly, genuinely touched that anyone did — and then goes straight back to
   weighing. No resolution, per the region and the slate.
6. **Closing beat.** Comfort as horror, held light to the last: Prosper wishes the Fool a
   pleasant day below, already reaching for the next deposit, perfectly, terribly content.

**[If WS_DEVIL_UNBOUND: …]** — Once the chains fall, Prosper is exactly the sort who
stays. His Calling continues; the ledger now records the few deposits of the few who
remained. He is still happy — if anything, freedom has made his contentment a *choice*,
which is somehow both better and worse. He may be counted among those who never walked
up (see `world.md`'s "some walk back down"), and the darkest-comedy line lands harder for
being, now, entirely voluntary.
