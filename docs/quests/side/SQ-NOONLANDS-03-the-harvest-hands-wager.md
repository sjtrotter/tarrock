# SQ-NOONLANDS-03 — The Harvest Hand's Wager

## Introduction

Two harvest hands have a sheaf-toss rivalry, and it has been tied — exactly, dead level —
every single day for three hundred years. Neither will admit noticing that this is
impossible; the impossibility is the joke, and the joke is the Stall wearing a friendly
face. This is the Noonlands' lightest quest, a bit of festival play with the Harvest hand
Calling loop, and its whole payoff is that the Fool's arrival is the first new thing to
happen here in three centuries: one honestly-thrown sheaf finally breaks a tie that was
never allowed to break.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Poll and Fenner Straw (characters.md §Regional named NPCs) are mid-
   contest at the festival grounds, cheerfully at each other's throats, each swearing that
   *today* is the day one of them finally pulls ahead. Their tally, they'll tell anyone,
   has been level "as long as we've kept count" — said without a flicker of noticing what
   that means.
2. **The stasis tell.** Watch a few rounds and the pattern is plain: every measure comes
   out equal, because in the Noonlands nothing is permitted to resolve. Their eternal tie
   is the frozen world in miniature, carried lightly, as a running gag two friends have
   built a friendship on.
3. **The Fool joins in.** A short sheaf-toss game (the Harvest hand Calling loop, or a
   bespoke variant) — the Fool competes, or coaches one of them, learning the toss.
4. **The break.** One honestly-thrown sheaf — the Fool's own, or one the Fool helps a
   competitor finally land clean — tips the tally at last. The first uneven score in
   three hundred years lands with an almost audible *click*.
5. **The delighted outrage.** Both are scandalized and thrilled in equal measure,
   immediately demanding a rematch, loudly blaming each other and the Fool and the wind.
   The honest beat under the comedy: for one second both go quiet — they *felt* something
   actually move — before papering it over with even louder bickering.
6. **`[If WS_SUN_UNBOUND]`.** With real days and seasons returned, the eternal tie was
   already coming undone on its own; the quest reframes as a single friendly match rather
   than the breaking of a curse, and the two are already keeping a score that moves. The
   gag retires gracefully into ordinary rivalry.
7. **Closing beat.** Poll and Fenner keep a *running* score now — one that changes, day to
   day — and become a recurring pair of bickering festival barks. Reward: coins and a
   small Coins Renown lift for the win.
