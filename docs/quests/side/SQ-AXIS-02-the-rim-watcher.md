# SQ-AXIS-02 — The Rim-Watcher

## Introduction

An old man has stood at the outermost edge of the Axis approach for as long as anyone can
remember, watching the still figure dance at the center, and has never once stepped
closer. Nothing gates him — nothing gates anyone; the Axis is always open — he simply
cannot make himself go in, afraid of what it will mean if the dancing ever stops. He asks
the Fool to cross the line he never could and come back and tell him, gently, what it
looked like. The Fool does not reach the center — that belongs to the journey's end — but
crosses far enough to see what a lifetime of standing back never allowed, and brings back
the one thing the old man actually wants, which is not the facts but the kindness.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Corvin Rathe (characters.md §Regional named NPCs) at his worn standing-
   spot on the rim — a patch of ground he's stood on so long it remembers his boots.
   Nothing stops him walking in. He just never has.
2. **His fear.** Not of the center, but of the dance *ending*. As long as the figure
   dances, the old man reasons, the world holds — and he's spent a life keeping watch as
   if the watching helped hold it. (Theme 3: the change he refuses is the one he most
   fears; he does not want the dancing to ever stop, and cannot bear to be the one who
   sees it.)
3. **The ask.** He cannot cross his own line. He asks the Fool to walk closer than he
   ever dared, and come back, and tell him — gently — what it looked like up close.
4. **The crossing.** The Fool walks the approach past Rathe's line: not to the center
   (that ground is MQ21's alone), but far enough to see what a lifetime of standing back
   never permitted — the figure near enough now to be a person and not a distant rumor,
   dancing, unhurried, not stopping. `[Reticence: the Fool observes only that it dances,
   that it is beautiful, that it does not stop — nothing of who, and nothing of what
   finishing would do.]`
5. **The telling.** The Fool reports back. `[Fool choice, ≤ 12 words: earnest — "Still
   dancing. Not tired of it. It's beautiful." / gentler-foolish — "Same lovely nonsense,
   just closer."]` The gift is entirely in the gentleness; the facts are almost beside the
   point.
6. **The ache and the laugh.** The honest ache: Rathe weeps — relieved, that it's still
   dancing and that he never had to be the one to watch it stop. The laugh: he immediately,
   stubbornly resumes his post as though nothing happened, a little easier in himself now.
   He still won't go in. He only needed someone to have gone *for* him.
7. **`[If CONFESSED]`.** The Fool now knows the dancing does end — when the Fool finishes
   the journey — and knows Rathe's fear is exactly, precisely right. The "gently" in the
   telling becomes deliberate, tender omission: the Fool gives him the truth of the beauty
   and withholds the truth of the ending. Never spoken; felt in what the Fool leaves out.
