# SQ-VEIL-01 — The Book That Isn't Finished

## Introduction

Deep in the Veil's sealed stacks, an under-librarian named Brother Fenwick Cray has built
decades of quiet faith on a single shelved volume he is certain proves the Reading can
never end. The player does not come looking for his book — they come hunting a *different*
lost text, following Pip's nose down aisles no one has walked in three centuries — and
stumble across Cray's proof by accident. The truth of it is gentler and sadder than any
prophecy: the book is simply *unfinished*. Its ink runs out mid-sentence, exactly as
everything else in the Veil does, and the man has mistaken a paralysis for a promise. The
quest's whole difficulty is not the finding but the telling — because Cray does not want
the thing pointed out to him kindly, or cruelly, or at all. He wants it left alone. A
quiet puzzle about a library that has confused its own stopped clock for the shape of
eternity.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** The Fool is tracing a lost index-text through the sealed stacks (a
   fetch-thread of Pip's Seek and careful, hush-bound movement past dozing archivists, in
   the Veil's stealth-and-riddle register). Brother Fenwick Cray (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), an under-librarian who has
   made this aisle his life, offers to help — for the price of the Fool hearing him out
   about *his* book first.
2. **The proof.** Cray shows the Fool the volume he has staked his faith on: a treatise
   he reads as demonstrating, chapter by patient chapter, that no Reading can ever reach
   its final card. It has kept him steady through three centuries of stopped time. He
   recites his favourite passage from memory, and it is genuinely beautiful.
3. **The complication.** Cross-checking against the index the Fool actually came for makes
   the plain thing plain: the book has no ending because it was never finished. The ink
   simply stops mid-sentence — the author caught by the Stall like everyone else, quill
   still on the page. What Cray reads as "proof the story cannot end" is a story that was
   interrupted, the same wound the whole Veil is nursing, bound in calf and shelved.
4. **The ache and the laugh.** The quest's real puzzle is what the Fool does with this.
   Cray, it turns out, half-knows already — he has simply never let anyone finish the
   sentence for him, which is its own small joke about a librarian afraid of an ending.
   The Fool can say nothing and take the lost index and go; can tell him gently and watch
   him absorb it; or can leave the book on its shelf, closed, the way he asked. The
   dialogue never marks a "correct" choice — leaving a man his comfort is a real answer
   here, not a failure.
5. **Closing beat.** Whatever the Fool chooses, nothing world-breaking turns on it: Cray's
   faith bends or holds at NPC level, his greetings soften or sharpen a shade, and the
   Fool walks out with the text they actually came for. Theme 1, worn very light — the
   unfinished book is the Stall in miniature, and an ending, offered or withheld, is the
   only mercy on the table.

  stay unfinished forever as the truer image? Recommend it stays unfinished.
