# SQ-CONFLUENCE-03 — The Tea That Never Steeps Right

## Introduction

A small comic caper against an entire region's metaphysics, homed at the Confluence and
playable in either state. Alchemist Comfrey Cross has been trying for three hundred years
to brew one perfect cup of tea, and is always interrupted at the ideal moment — because
nothing here is allowed to finish. The quest is the Fool helping her steal thirty
uninterrupted seconds from a city built to deny them. It is funny throughout and then,
quietly, it isn't: the tea was never really the point. The Mixer's-apprentice Calling
(`callings.md`) is a natural doorway, and the caper nods to MQ14's scald-and-frost hazard
without borrowing its fight.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Comfrey Cross (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) has chased one
   flawless cup since the night before the Stall, and every single attempt is snatched
   away at the perfect moment: a mixer bursts in mid-argument, a half-finished delivery
   arrives, the water itself hesitates in the kettle. She needs a conspirator.
2. **The plan.** Thirty uninterrupted seconds is the whole objective. The Fool clears the
   shop of would-be interrupters, muffles the door-bell, and learns to time the pour
   between the delta's alternating scald-mist and frost-spray bands (a light nod to
   MQ14's core hazard — cited, not reused as combat).
3. **The escalation (the comedy).** Every attempt is foiled by something exquisitely
   Confluence: a guild deputation, a "nearly finished" apprentice's blend that must be
   admired, a bridge-broker seeking a mediation. The city itself seems to conspire to
   keep the cup from ever being done.
4. **The honest beat.** Cornered by her own failing plan, Comfrey admits the tea was never
   really it. Three centuries in, she is chasing the *feeling of finishing anything at
   all* — the cup is a stand-in for an ending she is otherwise denied. Theme 1, said
   plainly and then not dwelt on.
5. **The near-miss (pre-unbinding).** While Temperance still holds, the best the caper can
   manage is twenty-eight seconds — and the region snatches the last two back at the
   brink. Comfrey laughs, genuinely devastated, and resolves to try again tomorrow. A pre-
   unbinding quest cannot truly resolve this; the metaphysics win, gently, and that is
   honest.

**[If WS_TEMPERANCE_UNBOUND: …]** — Once the delta drains and things may finish, Comfrey
finally gets her thirty seconds, and her cup. She drinks it. It is merely very good tea —
not a revelation, not a benediction, just a properly steeped brew — and she is quietly,
humanly disappointed, then laughs at herself for having wanted the tea to be more than
tea. The most human beat in the region, per the slate. She offers the Fool the second
cup, and the Fool can be earnest or dry — "You should drink the first one." / "Was it
worth three hundred years?" (≤12 words, one earnest option).
