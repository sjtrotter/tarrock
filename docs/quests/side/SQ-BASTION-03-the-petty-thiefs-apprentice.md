# SQ-BASTION-03 — The Petty Thief's Apprentice

## Introduction

With the Emperor unbound, petty crime returns to the Bastion for the first time in three
hundred years — and the very first thief is spectacularly, comprehensively bad at it,
because there is no living tradition left to learn from. Catching "Sixpence" Loft
mid-fumble opens a gentle comic choice: report him, scold him, or quietly teach him to at
least be competent. Freedom, it turns out, needs practice — at being bad as much as
being good.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** A commotion at a market stall — the Bastion's first theft in three
   centuries, the same stall MQ04's aftermath already noted. The thief, "Sixpence" Loft
   (characters.md §Regional named NPCs), is still standing there holding the stolen apple,
   baffled that "take thing, leave" did not work.
2. **The comedy.** Loft has reasoned theft out from first principles and gotten every
   part of it wrong — announces his intentions, apologizes mid-grab, tries to haggle with
   the person he is robbing. The guards, equally out of practice, have no idea how to
   arrest him. Nobody in the Bastion has done this dance in 300 years.
3. **The honest beat.** Loft isn't hungry and isn't cruel. He is testing whether the new
   freedom is *real* — whether a man can do a wrong thing and the sky stay up. He
   half-wants to be caught, just to find where the new edges are.
4. **The choice.**
   - **Report him.** The guards, quietly relieved to have ordinary work again, process
     him gently; Loft is almost grateful for the structure.
   - **Scold him.** The Fool sends him off shamed; he swears off it, unconvincingly.
   - **Teach him.** The Fool — nobody, from nowhere (theme 4), the one figure with no
     stake in the Bastion's order — gives Loft the lesson the city has no one else to
     give: that competent thieving is mostly knowing what isn't worth stealing.
5. **The turn / the laugh.** Whichever route, Loft concludes crime is far more work than
   the Bastion made it look and drifts toward an honest trade out of sheer laziness — or
   settles into being a harmlessly, cheerfully terrible pickpocket the market adopts as a
   mascot. [If CONFESSED: told what the Fool truly is, Loft asks why bother teaching
   anyone anything if it's all ending. Earnest Fool option: "Because it isn't ending yet."]
6. **Closing.** Freedom, the Bastion is discovering, has to be practiced. Loft becomes a
   recurring, hapless fixture at the market — proof the city is finally alive enough to
   have small sins in it.

Rewards, if any: recovered or gifted coins; a small rise in the local suit's Renown.
