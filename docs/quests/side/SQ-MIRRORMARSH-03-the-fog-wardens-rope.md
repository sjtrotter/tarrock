# SQ-MIRRORMARSH-03 — The Fog-Warden's Rope

## Introduction

A small, whimsical quest that plays along the safe edge of the Mirrormarsh, on the guide-rope
the fog-warden walks to bring the lost back out — no true light required, because this never
ventures into the lying interior (that hard gate, per `world.md`, still guards the deep marsh
beyond the rope's reach). The fog-warden's guide-rope has one frayed, badly-knotted section
she flatly refuses to replace, on the superstition that a *perfect* rope would stop working —
the marsh, she reasons, likes to be needed as much as anyone does. The quest is a gentle,
funny turn on care disguised as ritual, and a soft rhyme with the whole game's thesis that a
thing has to be able to fray a little to still be alive.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Nettle Vance (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), the Mirrormarsh's fog-warden, walks her rope-line at the marsh's edge, guiding the
   fog-lost back to solid ground. The Fool, ever helpful, notices one span of the rope is worn
   thin and clumsily re-knotted, and offers to replace it properly.
2. **The refusal.** Nettle won't hear of it. A perfect rope, she says with total conviction,
   would stop working — the marsh only keeps its bargain with a warden it still needs, and a
   flawless line would insult it. She has kept that frayed span deliberately for longer than
   she'll say.
3. **The complication.** The Fool can humour the superstition, argue it (dry, doomed), or ask
   to walk the line and see for themselves. Walking it in the murk reveals the frayed knot is
   how Nettle *finds her place* on the rope when the fog is thickest — the flaw is a landmark,
   the one spot her hands know blind. The superstition is a story wrapped around a real, practical,
   quietly moving thing.
4. **The honest beat.** Pressed gently, Nettle admits the knot is older than her tenure — she
   inherited the rope, and the flaw, from the warden who taught her, and re-frays it by hand each
   season rather than lose the one part of the line that was tied by someone she loved. Care,
   dressed up as superstition, so she never has to say the tender thing outright.
5. **The laugh.** She is mortified to have been drawn into saying it, and covers immediately: the
   marsh is *needy*, that's all, and if the Fool tells anyone she got sentimental over a bit of
   frayed rope she'll walk them into a bog personally. It is not remotely a threat; the fog-lost
   adore her.
6. **Closing beat.** The Fool learns to walk the rope by the frayed span, the flaw as a friend.
   Nettle keeps her imperfect line exactly as it is — the rope that works *because* it isn't
   perfect, tended by hand against the day it might finally give.

## Open questions

- The surname **Vance/Vane** recurs across regions (Sorrel Vance in MQ16, Nettle Vance
  here, Marrow Vance in the Wheelhouse, Perpetua and Prosper Vane elsewhere). The one
  same-region clash — the Spire's Harrow Vane — was renamed **Harrow Brock** at the
  characters.md promotion; the rest stands as common-surname texture unless the design
  lead rules otherwise.
- Whether walking Nettle's rope should be a genuine short traversal beat (finding the frayed span
  by feel in low visibility) or dialogue-only. Recommend the former — it lets the player *feel*
  the flaw-as-landmark rather than be told it.
