# SQ-DIM-03 — The Lamplighter's Count

## Introduction

Old Wick Hollin has walked the Dim's dusk routes so long the wicks know his hand, and for
decades he has privately kept a second tally alongside the lighting: every lamp still
burning on his round, counted and re-counted, in the quiet certainty that the number
*means* something — if it ever, just once, comes out even. It never has. The player is
invited along for a single round, expecting a lamplighting errand, and instead walks a man
slowly toward the thing he has spent decades not-quite-admitting: the counting was never
about the number. It is the Dim's gentlest small quest — one old fellow, one dark
mountain, one companionable evening, and a truth set down as lightly as a lamp.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Taking up (or shadowing) the Lamplighter Calling (`callings.md`), the
   Fool falls in with Wick Hollin (`characters.md` §Regional named NPCs — promoted in the
   parallel slate change), who mutters numbers between lamps and waves off questions about
   it. Pressed kindly, he admits the count: he's after an even total, and the Dim has
   never once given him one.
   - Fool (earnest): "What happens if it's even?" / (foolish): "I'll blow one out to
     help."
2. **The round.** Walking the route lamp by lamp through the permanent dusk, the Fool
   lighting or re-trimming as Wick counts aloud — a slow, companionable rhythm, no combat,
   the region's whole mood in one evening's work.
3. **The complication.** As the count climbs, Wick's certainty frays: a lamp gutters, then
   another catches, and the number he wants keeps sliding away by ones. He's chased it for
   decades; he lets the Fool see, for the first time, how much he's staked on a sum that
   was never going to balance in a world that cannot change.
4. **The turn.** Somewhere past the last lamp, Wick stops counting — not because it came
   out even, but because, saying the numbers aloud to another person for once, he hears
   himself and knows. The even total was never the point. The point was the round, the
   dark, and having something to do in it that felt like it mattered.
5. **The ache and the laugh.** He admits it plainly and then, mortified at the size of the
   confession, deflects into a lamplighter's grumble that the Fool's re-lit wicks have
   "ruined his figures for a week" — the honest beat and the laugh in the same breath.
6. **Closing beat.** Wick keeps walking the round — he always will — but the muttered
   counting eases out of his barks, replaced by talk. A Cups Renown gain (for the company
   more than the work) or a cosmetic lamplighter's flourish may reward the evening.
   - `[If WS_HERMIT_UNBOUND]` If the Hermit is later unbound, Wick's round shortens as
     fewer lamps are needed (`callings.md`); he tells the Fool, dry, that at least now the
     count comes out lower — and that he doesn't count it anymore anyway.
