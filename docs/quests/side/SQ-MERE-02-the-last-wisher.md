# SQ-MERE-02 — The Last Wisher

## Introduction

This quest opens only once the Fool has unbound the Star (`WS_STAR_UNBOUND`) and the Mere's
wish-wells have woken. It follows a pilgrim who has kept vigil beside one single wish-light
for three hundred years — his own, made the night before the Stall, for something he can
barely bring himself to name — and who now, with the wells stirring at last, must face the
one outcome he never planned for: being answered. There is no danger here and no combat; the
Mere stays gentle. The quest is about the particular grief of getting the thing you waited
for, and discovering that "the one who waits" was the only self you had left.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Thomlin Reeve (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), an elderly pilgrim, has not left his place beside one specific wish-light in living
   memory. Now that the wells have woken, the others rejoice; Thomlin has gone very quiet. His
   wish is stirring, and after three hundred years of wanting it, he is terrified of it landing.
2. **The complication.** He can barely say what he wished for. Drawn out gently, it comes: not
   riches, not the dead returned — forgiveness, for something old he did before the Stall, from
   someone he wronged and never made it right with. He has waited three centuries not for the
   wish to be granted but, he half-admits, for it to stay safely *un*-granted, where hope can't
   be disappointed.
3. **The answer.** The woken wish-network delivers it — small and true, the way real answers
   are: a letter, at last carried through, from the one he wronged, forgiving him plainly and
   without ceremony, written long ago and only now able to arrive. The Fool may be the one who
   fetches it the final stretch, or simply the one who sits with him while it comes.
4. **The ache.** He is forgiven. And he does not know how to be a forgiven man, because he has
   been "the one who's waiting" for so long that it became the whole shape of him. The vigil was
   never only about the wish; it was a life, and the wish being answered has quietly ended it.
   Theme 3 in its purest key — the answer everyone would call a happy ending, and it costs him
   the only self he knew.
5. **The laugh.** In the middle of it, Thomlin manages a dry one: three hundred years of vigil,
   and the reply fits in an envelope; he could murder whoever runs the post. The joke is how he
   keeps his feet. The Fool cannot fix any of this and is not asked to — only to stay, which the
   Mere has already taught (MQ17) is the whole job.
6. **Closing beat.** Thomlin lets the wish-light go dark — his own, tended and answered and
   finished — and stands up from a place he has not stood up from in three centuries, with
   nowhere in particular to go and, for the first time, the freedom to find one.
