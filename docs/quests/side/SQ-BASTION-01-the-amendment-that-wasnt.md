# SQ-BASTION-01 — The Amendment That Wasn't

## Introduction

A small comic tour of the Bastion's bureaucratic machine, built around a want so modest
it should take a minute and instead has taken three hundred years. Goodwife Nan Ostler
wants one word corrected on her late husband's memorial plaque — the wrong guild, a
clerk's slip made the week of the Stall and frozen ever since. In a city whose law
cannot amend, even a spelling error is eternal. The Fool arrives as the one person in the
Bastion who has never been taught that "it cannot be changed" is the end of a sentence.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** In a queue outside the Registry, Nan Ostler (characters.md §Regional
   named NPCs) intercepts the Fool with a petition worn soft as cloth. Her husband's
   plaque names the wrong guild; she wants no pardon, no honor, no restitution — only his
   trade spelled right. She has filed the same request every lawful day for 300 years.
2. **The law.** No plaque may be re-cut without an Edict of Correction; no Edict issues
   without amendment; amendment is forbidden. Each clerk is scrupulously correct and
   entirely helpless, and each sends the Fool to the next — a perfect closed loop of
   stamps that cancel one another out.
3. **The absurd tour.** One functionary (unnamed walk-on) holds the exact stamp required
   but no authority to ink it; another holds the authority but the wrong stamp. The
   comedy is the machine's flawless futility: a city that runs to the minute cannot fix
   a single letter.
4. **The honest beat.** Nan admits the correction was never quite the point. The morning
   she stops filing is the morning she admits he is thirty years gone and she is still
   here, still queuing. The errand is her grief, kept moving so it never has to land.
5. **The seam (pre-unbinding).** The Fool finds the one legal gap: not *amending* the old
   Edict but adding a new, separately-Edicted brass plate beside it, naming the true
   guild. The law forbids changing; it never forbade *adding*. The plaque now carries the
   error and the truth together, side by side — the Bastion's stasis honored to the
   letter and quietly defeated by it.
6. **Closing.** Nan reads the new plate, furious it took a stranger to see the gap and
   grateful past words. The next morning, for the first time in three centuries, she
   files nothing. [If WS_EMPEROR_UNBOUND: with the law free to amend, the fix is trivial
   — a clerk simply re-cuts the plaque — and the quest becomes Nan learning her whole
   ritual is suddenly over; the ache moves from the loophole to the empty morning where
   the queuing used to be.]

Rewards, if any: modest coins and a small rise in Renown with the Bastion's local suit —
nothing gated, nothing mechanical.
