# SQ-CHANTRY-03 — The Wedding That Waits

## Introduction

A young Cups-culture couple, Wren and Sorrel Tamsin, want to be married in the Chantry —
but doctrine holds that weddings happen only at the sacred hour, and the sacred hour, since
the Stall, never actually arrives. They have been "nearly married" for years, their banns
read and re-read until the parchment has gone soft. Finding the loophole, or simply the
kindness, that lets the ceremony happen anyway is a lovely small rehearsal of what
unbinding the Hierophant later does for the whole region — and a quiet contest between the
Cups way (feeling) and the Chantry way (form).

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Wren and Sorrel Tamsin (characters.md §Regional named NPCs) greet the
   Fool with a blessing, Cups-fashion, and their long gentle grievance: doctrine permits
   weddings only at the sacred hour, and the bells have never reached it. Their banns have
   been read so many times the parchment is soft as cloth.
2. **The doctrine wall.** The Chantry's eternal single hour means the sacred hour is
   forever coming and never arriving. The officiants are sympathetic and immovable — the
   rule is the rule, and the rule is the town's entire weather.
3. **The comedy.** Increasingly earnest attempts to reach the sacred hour: arguing
   theology with a sub-deacon, hunting an older rite, trying to catch the bells at the
   "right" toll that never once differs. Cups warmth breaking itself against Chantry
   doctrine, again and again, cheerfully.
4. **The honest beat.** The couple confess they are not even certain they are still
   waiting for the *hour* — they are waiting for permission to stop waiting. Three
   centuries of "nearly" has become its own comfortable trap, and the wedding frightens
   them a little now, precisely because it would *finish* something.
5. **The kindness (pre-unbinding).** The Fool finds not a loophole in the hour but one in
   the doctrine's spirit: an old provision that a marriage needs only witnesses and vows
   freely given — the sacred hour being tradition, not law. A small ceremony, off the
   register, witnessed by the Fool and Pip and whoever is near. Feeling beats form,
   gently, the Cups way.
6. **Closing.** Wren and Sorrel are married — quietly, without the bells, held by
   witnesses rather than doctrine: a rehearsal in miniature of the region's whole
   liberation. [If WS_HIEROPHANT_UNBOUND: the bells now ring weddings freely and the
   couple could have the full ceremony they always wanted — but they keep the small
   witnessed vows they'd already have taken, the sacred hour arriving at last as a
   blessing on a marriage that was made without it.]

Rewards, if any: a rise in Cups Renown (hospitality and feeling prized); optionally a
cosmetic wedding-favor or festival outfit — nothing gated.
