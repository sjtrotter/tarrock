# SQ-UNDERVAULT-02 — The One Who Walked Back Down

## Introduction

A post-unbinding grief piece, available once the Fool has broken the Undervault's chains
(`WS_DEVIL_UNBOUND`). The Corrigan family are overjoyed to have their son Wend freed —
and devastated three days later when he quietly walks back down into the pit, unable to
bear a life without the comfort his contract promised. The family cannot understand it,
and the Fool cannot fix it. The quest's only mercy is making sure the goodbye, this time,
gets said properly. It is the family's-eye view of the world-state note that "some walk
back down," and a distinct case from MQ15's Merrow Slate — the Undervault holds more than
one such story on purpose.

## Beats

1. **The gladness.** With the chains fallen, the Corrigan family celebrate: their son
   Wend Corrigan (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) is free of the Undervault after
   years bound. For three days the reunion is everything they hoped.
2. **The re-descent.** On the third day Wend walks calmly back down into the pit and
   re-latches a chain that no longer holds him — not coerced, not tricked (Old Nick is
   unbound; nobody is holding anyone now), simply unable to live in a world without the
   comfort the contract gave. His family are gutted and baffled.
3. **The ask.** The Corrigans cannot follow him down — they cannot bear the pit, or the
   answer. They ask the Fool, who goes where others won't, to bring him back, or at least
   to learn why.
4. **The descent.** The Fool finds Wend calm and clear-eyed, wholly himself, and entirely
   decided. He explains without self-pity: the surface is too large and too cold, and the
   chain is warm, and he would rather a small certain life than a wide frightening one.
   Nothing the Fool says will move him. The Fool cannot fix this, and the quest never
   pretends otherwise.
5. **The mercy.** The one thing the Fool *can* broker is a proper goodbye — getting the
   family and Wend into a single room to say what a silent re-descent left unsaid, instead
   of losing him twice. The Fool's lines stay short and honest — "Come up and say it to
   them." / "He isn't coming up." (≤12 words, one earnest option).
6. **The laugh in the sad scene.** Wend, absurdly and tenderly, asks his mother to bring
   him a very specific pie on holidays — he isn't dead, only staying — and she agrees,
   furious and weeping at once, already arguing about the crust. Grotesque, human, and
   exactly the tone bar.
7. **Closing beat.** The goodbye is said. Wend stays; the family climb back up. Nothing is
   resolved and the quest refuses to resolve it — per `world.md`, walking back down is
   permanent and unresolved on purpose. The Fool delivers Wend's answer, not the family's
   hoped-for one.
