# SQ-NOONLANDS-02 — The Reveller Afraid of the Dark

## Introduction

The first sunset in three hundred years has come and gone (MQ19), and the Noonlands'
brightest, most joyful resident is quietly its most frightened. Sunny Loft has never
once seen night, never needed sleep, never been tired — she has built a whole radiant
self out of an endless noon that asked nothing of her but delight. Now the light is
going, and it is asking. This quest has no puzzle and no fight; it is a sitting-with. The
player's job is company, and the quiet discovery that company is enough.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Post-sunset, the eternal harvest festival gutters into its first real
   evening. Sunny Loft (characters.md §Regional named NPCs), harvest-hand and festival
   dancer, is still dancing — too brightly, too fast, insisting to anyone near that she's
   perfectly fine as the gold drains out of the sky.
2. **The tell.** She isn't afraid of the dark, exactly. She's afraid of the *rest* it so
   plainly wants from her. She has never been tired, because there was never an evening
   to be tired in — and the tiredness arriving now, all three centuries of it at once,
   terrifies her more than any monster could. (Theme 2: the endless-noon reveller is a
   role that ate the person inside it. Theme 3: the Sun's mercy still costs someone
   ordinary something real.)
3. **Being present (player choice).** Keep her dancing a while longer to put off the
   dark; sit with her at the festival's edge; or walk her out to the fields to watch the
   horizon do the thing it has never done. There is no wrong choice — each is just a
   different shape of *staying*.
4. **The first night.** Full dark falls: the Noonlands' first true night.
   `[If WS_STAR_UNBOUND]`: the Mere's returned stars are already waiting overhead, and
   Sunny has a whole sky to be astonished by. `[If NOT WS_STAR_UNBOUND]`: the dark is
   plain and starless, ordinary and enormous — different, equally overwhelming, per the
   order-independence rule (world.md §Interaction rules).
5. **The laugh inside the sad.** Sunny yawns for the first time in her life and is
   *outraged* — genuine, funny indignation that her own body would betray her like this.
   The honest beat underneath: she cries a little, not from fear, but from the relief of
   finally having a word for how long she has been tired.
6. **Enough.** She sleeps, or nearly. It turns out sitting with her through one sunset
   was the entire task. Afterward her barks gain an evening register — she becomes a
   dusk-and-lamplight presence as much as a noon one, and is, quietly, glad of it.
7. **`[If CONFESSED]`.** The Fool knows by now exactly what all this learning-to-rest is
   rehearsal for. Teaching someone to let an evening arrive lands under the shadow of the
   larger ending the Fool is walking toward — played tenderly, felt in the Fool's
   restraint, and never once spoken aloud to Sunny.
