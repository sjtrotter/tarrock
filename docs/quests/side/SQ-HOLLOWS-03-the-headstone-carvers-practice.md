# SQ-HOLLOWS-03 — The Headstone Carver's Practice

## Introduction

Mortality has come back to the world, and one Hollows stonemason has decided the best way
to befriend it is to carve practice epitaphs — for the not-yet-dead, for cheerful
neighbors who commissioned their own as a lark, and, gladly, for herself. It is funnier
than it sounds and sadder than it looks, and it is the Hollows' warmest quest: a bit of
choosing words over stone with a woman who can finally joke about dying because, for three
hundred years, she couldn't. If asked, she'll carve one for the Fool too — the single best
joke in the region.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Bess Corrigan (characters.md §Regional named NPCs), stonemason, is
   cheerfully chiseling a headstone for a neighbor who is very much alive and standing
   right there, having commissioned their own — a lark, and a comfort, now that dying is a
   thing that happens again.
2. **The practice.** Since Death was unbound, Bess has carved practice epitaphs by the
   dozen — her way of making peace with a mortality that was frozen clean out of the world
   for her entire life until now. (Theme 1: endings as the mercy that makes a life a
   life.)
3. **Lending a hand.** The Fool helps — hauling stone, or choosing the words for a few
   commissions (a small writing/choice beat), each epitaph a tiny, fond character portrait
   of a Hollows resident. The work is warmer and gentler than a graveyard has any right to
   be.
4. **Her own stone.** Bess shows the Fool the one she is carving for herself, and it is
   genuinely funny and genuinely brave. The honest beat under the comedy: the only reason
   she *can* joke is that for three centuries she could not, and the joke is precisely how
   she is glad to be mortal again.
5. **The offer.** She'll carve one for the Fool, if asked. `[Fool choice, ≤ 12 words:
   earnest — "I'd be honored. Make it kind." / foolish — "Only if it rhymes."]` The
   epitaph she gives is the best joke in the Hollows — warm, teasing, wholly in-world, and
   about exactly what Bess can see: a stranger who turned up with a dog and a rose. It says
   nothing she couldn't know, because she cannot.
6. **`[If CONFESSED]`.** The Fool now knows their own ending is stranger than any
   headstone Bess could imagine. Accepting a cheerful joke epitaph while carrying that
   knowledge is played bittersweet — felt in the Fool's quiet, never spoken to Bess, and
   the epitaph itself is authored to land as pure warmth, never as an ironic wink at the
   twist.
7. **Reward and Calling hook.** Bess becomes a recurring, fond presence with workplace
   barks; she works the plots too, so the Groundskeeper Calling (callings.md) gains her as
   a co-worker voice. Reward: the carved keepsake as a cosmetic trinket, and/or a Coins
   Renown lift for the craft.
