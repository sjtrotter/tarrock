# SQ-DIVIDE-02 — The Ferryman's Gossip

## Introduction

Ferry hand Sculley Marsh has poled the Divide's gossip-ferry so long that he knows every
household's business on both banks better than they know it themselves — he was the
canyon's true bridge for three hundred years, long before the stone one existed. When the
Betrothed finally choose and the bridge completes, foot traffic simply walks across, and
Sculley's boat sits idle. He is quietly, genuinely proud of the marriage he helped nudge
along, and quietly bereft of the only thing he was ever needed for. A study in the good
thing that costs someone their whole purpose.

## Beats

1. **The hook (post-unbinding).** The bridge is finished; the ferry landing is deserted.
   Sculley Marsh (characters.md §Regional named NPCs), who spent his life poling
   passengers and news across the gorge, sits in a boat nobody needs, hailing travelers
   who now walk across the span above his head.
2. **Offices eat people (theme 2).** Sculley was never merely a boatman — he was the one
   channel news crossed the canyon by, for three centuries. Without the crossing, he is
   not sure what he is. The ferry made the man; the bridge unmade the office.
3. **Freedom isn't wanted (theme 3).** The whole Divide celebrates the bridge. For Sculley
   it is the end of a small world — the good thing that took his purpose — and he is
   careful never to say he wishes it hadn't come, because he helped it come and he is
   proud of that.
4. **His pride (branch-flavored).** Over the years Sculley carried a hundred small
   messages that nudged the two towns toward peace, and toward the wedding itself. He
   wants the Fool to know his gossip *mattered* — that the marriage the region cheers had
   a boatman's thumb quietly on the scale.
   - [If WS_DIVIDE_EASTMARRIED: he ferried the groom west-to-east more times than anyone
     counted; the bride's chapel bell that finally rang, he swears, rang partly on his oar.]
   - [If WS_DIVIDE_WESTMARRIED: he carried the bride's letters west so often he could
     recite them; the West hall's ceremony, he'll tell you, floated over on his ferry
     first.]
5. **The choice / the help.** The Fool can help Sculley find the new shape of his knowing
   — taking up the bridge's tollkeeping (the Ferry-hand Calling's own post-MQ06 successor),
   where he can still greet everyone who crosses — or simply listen while he grieves the
   oar. No fix erases the loss; presence is the mercy the quest actually offers.
6. **Closing.** Sculley draws his boat up the bank for the last time, or takes the
   toll-house, depending — either way keeping his true trade, which was never rowing but
   *knowing*. His barks move up onto the bridge. [If CONFESSED: Sculley, who hears
   everything eventually, already knows what the Fool is, and ferries them across one last
   time for free — "for the one taking us all over."]

Rewards, if any: modest coins; a small rise in the local suit's Renown.
