# SQ-SPIRE-02 — The Spotter's Last Watch

## Introduction

This quest exists only after the Spire has fallen (`WS_TOWER_UNBOUND`), and it refuses to
let the fall be simply triumphant. The player returns to a changed skyline and finds two
losses waiting at the wreckage that do not cancel each other out: a man whose entire self
was the disaster, now that the disaster is over, and a family whose home the real, falling,
non-metaphorical debris actually crushed. The quest's whole job is to sit in the space
between "the tower is down at last" and "and this is what it cost," and to help without
pretending either loss makes the other smaller. It is one of the Spire's quiet, hard-won
graces — Theme 3, freedom hurts someone ordinary, made specific.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Harrow Brock (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), the Spire's foremost bell-watch veteran, stands in the ruin of his own watch-post,
   still narrating the collapse to tourists who are no longer there. For three hundred years
   he was the region's leading expert on a catastrophe that never finished happening. It has
   now finished, and taken his subject, his post, and his purpose with it in an afternoon.
2. **The other loss.** Downhill, a family is digging their home out from under the actual
   fallen masonry — the "lightning bell" Harrow tended his whole life warned of a *strike*,
   never a *fall*, and nobody ever thought to warn anyone of the thing that finally happened.
   His three centuries of vigilance protected them from precisely nothing.
3. **The complication.** Harrow wants absolution the family cannot give him, and the family
   is not cruel about it — they are simply busy grieving a roof and have no spare grief for
   his identity. The Fool cannot broker forgiveness here, because nobody in it has done a
   deliberate wrong; there is only a disaster, and two different holes it left.
4. **The work.** The Fool can help the family salvage and begin to rebuild (honest, physical,
   thankless), and separately help Harrow toward the one role the changed world still has for
   him — the bell-watch's living-world successor: storm-spotter, now that `WS_TOWER_UNBOUND`
   has put real thunderstorms into the weather rotation. His old skill, aimed at last at
   something that can actually be warned of.
5. **The ache and the laugh.** Harrow's first genuine storm-warning is *correct*, and lands
   to a town that has never once had a reason to heed him — half of them ignore it, get
   rained on, and blame him anyway. It is very funny, and it is also the first time in three
   hundred years his watching saved somebody a soaking. The honest beat sits right beside it:
   the family's home is still gone, and a new job for Harrow does not un-gone it.
6. **Closing beat.** Harrow keeps the storm-watch. He does not get told he is forgiven,
   because there was never a crime — only a long life spent guarding against the wrong sky.
   The family's rebuild is begun, not finished. The quest ends with both facts true at once,
   and does not resolve either into the other.
