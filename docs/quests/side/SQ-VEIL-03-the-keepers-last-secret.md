# SQ-VEIL-03 — The Keeper's Last Secret

## Introduction

For three hundred years Sister Marrow has guarded one particular drawer, and only that
drawer — never saying what is inside it, the withholding the entire shape of her days.
Then the Fool unbinds the High Priestess, the Veil's mist lifts, and every hidden door in
the cloister becomes visible to anyone who walks past — including Marrow's drawer, which
now opens itself in the open, its secret simply *there* to be read. And the secret is
small: old love letters between two archivists dead for centuries, a modest tenderness
kept behind lock and vow. Marrow is not angry that it is out. She is grieving a purpose.
The quest finds her on the far side of the only job she ever had, learning what a keeper
is once there is nothing left to keep. This quest is available only after MQ02 ("Between
the Pillars").

## Beats

1. **The hook.** With the mist gone (`WS_PRIESTESS_UNBOUND`), the cloister's sealed doors
   and drawers stand open the length of the stacks. The Fool finds Sister Marrow (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) standing before
   one such drawer — hers — that has swung open of its own accord, its contents plain to
   every passing eye for the first time in three centuries. She is not reading them. She
   is just looking at the open drawer.
2. **The secret.** The drawer holds a bundle of love letters: two archivists, long dead,
   who wrote to each other in the margins of the work and never once said it aloud. That is
   all. No prophecy, no danger, no forbidden knowledge — a private, ordinary sweetness that
   Marrow was set to guard so long ago she has outlived everyone who knew why it mattered.
3. **The complication.** Marrow's whole self is the keeping. Ask her about the letters and
   she can tell you nothing of the lovers — she never read them; reading was never the job.
   *Guarding* was. Now the mist that made her necessary is gone, the drawer keeps itself
   open, and the office that ate three centuries of her (theme 2) has quietly dissolved out
   from under her (theme 3). She is bereft in a way she has no words for.
4. **The ache and the laugh.** The Fool can read the letters aloud to her — so the keeper
   finally learns what she kept — or leave them private one last time, or help her decide
   what becomes of them now anyone may. The dry laugh: told the letters are just two shy
   people in love, Marrow is almost offended at how *small* the thing she guarded turns out
   to be, and then, helplessly, moved by exactly that. The quest does not hand her a new
   purpose; it only sits with her losing the old one.
5. **Closing beat.** However it lands, the outcome is NPC-level: Marrow's greetings soften
   from a keeper's clipped hush toward something more uncertain and more human, and the
   letters find a resting place of the Fool's choosing — read into the open, or closed and
   left be. A keeper on the far side of keeping, learning, slowly, to be an ordinary nun in
   a cloister that finally dreams.
