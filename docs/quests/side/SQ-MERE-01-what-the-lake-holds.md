# SQ-MERE-01 — What the Lake Holds

## Introduction

A gentle quest for the game's gentlest region, and a small love letter to the Spread's
hydrology rule: the truth that a world which looks completely stopped never quite is. The
Mere honours its own stillness absolutely — Fortune doesn't charge here, Blanks don't come,
and nothing in this quest is settled by a raised staff — so this is a quest of diving,
looking, and following water where it actually goes. The player helps the lake's wish-tender
answer a question three centuries old: when a wish sinks into the Mere, does it go anywhere,
or does it simply sit at the bottom forever? The answer, findable by anyone patient enough to
follow the water downhill, is quietly the most hopeful thing in the region.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Isolde Fenn (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), wish-tender of the Mere, trims the wicks of the wish-lights and muses aloud to the
   Fool: the lake is one long held breath, and she has wondered for three hundred years whether
   the coins and wishes dropped into it go *anywhere* — or whether she has spent a lifetime
   tending a bottom that only ever fills.
2. **The dive.** Within the region's no-combat rules, the Fool goes into the water — slow,
   weightless, unhurried, the star's reflection above and the sunk wishes glinting below. Pip
   settles at the jetty's end and waits, at ease, the calm dog he always is here. This is
   exploration as meditation, not challenge.
3. **The discovery.** The lake is not a sealed basin. Low on its western flank, exactly where
   gravity says an outlet should be, a modest spring-fed seep draws water out and away — so
   slow it is nearly invisible, but real. The Mere has an inlet and an outlet, a shape water
   could genuinely have held. The stillness was always a fiction over a current.
4. **The proof.** Tracing the seep's reckoning with Isolde, they work out the arithmetic: about
   one coin's worth of wish leaves the Mere every year, carried by that thread of water toward
   a marsh three days south. For three centuries, a single wish a year has, quietly, been
   *going somewhere* — the one region built entirely around looking stopped was never fully
   stopped at all.
5. **The ache and the laugh.** Isolde takes it two ways at once. Comfort — her tending was
   never pointless; something always left. And a small vertigo — if wishes have been leaving
   all along, then endings have been happening here all along, gently, under the held breath.
   The laugh: three hundred years of profound theology, and the answer was "the lake has a
   drain, love." She'll never quite forgive the water for being so ordinary about it.
6. **Closing beat.** Isolde tends the lights the same as ever, but tells the pilgrims now,
   when they ask, that yes — their wishes go somewhere. She has followed the water and knows.
   - `[If WS_STAR_UNBOUND: the wish-wells have woken; the seep runs a little freer, and the`
     `year's coin-worth becomes a season's. The lake was leaking hope by the thimbleful; now`
     `it pours. Isolde finds this more moving than she can say, and says a joke instead.]`

## Open questions

- The "marsh three days south" is left unnamed to avoid inventing geography. Given the Mere's
  south-westerly position (`world.md` §Layout), the plausible real terminus is the Stillmarsh
  directly south, or the Mirrormarsh's wetlands — a call for `world.md` if the seep's outfall
  is ever placed on the map. Recommend leaving it an unnamed distant marsh unless a downstream
  quest wants the connection.
- Whether the dive should surface any collectible (a Scattered Deck card, a rose grafting at
  the outlet) or stay purely a looking-quest. Recommend a single rose grafting at the seep as
  a fitting, non-combat reward; confirm against the grafting-source pass in `progression.md`.
