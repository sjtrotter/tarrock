# SQ-DIM-02 — The One Who Stayed

## Introduction

When the Hermit is unbound, the stars return over the Dim and the lost travelers finally
come down from the hills — all of them but one. The Corvenna family's long-missing son is
not among the returned, and they ask the Fool to go up and find out why. He is not dead,
not trapped, not lost: the player finds Ansel Corvenna alive, well, and quietly at the
center of an entire second life he built on the mountain everyone assumed had taken him.
Coming home would unmake everything he has become; not coming home leaves a three-hundred-
year vigil without its ending. The Fool cannot fix this, and the quest does not pretend
otherwise — it only carries Ansel's answer down the mountain, his answer, not one the
family or the Fool made kinder for them.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Post-`WS_HERMIT_UNBOUND`, the Dim's lost-traveler chain has emptied the
   hills — except the Corvenna family (unnamed elder relatives at their shack) are still
   watching the trail for a son who hasn't appeared. They ask the Fool, plainly, to bring
   him down.
   - Fool (earnest): "I'll find him. I can't promise more." / (foolish): "He's probably
     just slow."
2. **The climb.** With stars back to steer by (`world.md`), the once-blind high slopes are
   navigable; the Fool follows the family's old landmarks up to where the boy was last
   seen — and finds, instead of a grave, a tidy path, a mended fence, a life in use.
3. **The find.** Ansel Corvenna (`characters.md` §Regional named NPCs — promoted in the
   parallel slate change) is grown old on the mountain and entirely himself here: a home,
   a craft, a routine of dusk-work he chose and kept. He was never lost. He simply never
   came back, and let the world call that the same thing because it was easier for
   everyone, including him.
4. **The complication.** He is not cruel and not cold. He knows exactly what the family
   below has done with the years — the vigil, the watching trail — and he grieves it. But
   the mountain is where he became someone, and going down would ask him to be the boy
   who left, a person who no longer exists. He asks the Fool to carry his answer, and to
   carry it *true*.
5. **The ache and the laugh.** Ansel's request has one dry, self-aware joke in it — that
   he's had three hundred years to write the letter home and still can't find the first
   line — under a real, old grief that the freedom to come down is not the same as wanting
   to. The Fool bears the weight of choosing to deliver it honestly.
6. **Closing beat.** The Fool tells the family what Ansel said, unsoftened. The vigil ends
   — not in reunion, but in an answer, which is its own kind of ending. The family's barks
   settle from watching to mourning-that-can-finally-begin; nothing is resolved so much as
   allowed to close. No `WS_*` flag; the outcome lives entirely in these NPCs.

## Open questions

- This shares its shape with the Mirrormarsh's "The One Who Didn't Want to Go Home"
  (`SLATE.md`, XVIII). Confirm the two are written as deliberate echoes of one motif
  (someone the world calls lost who chose to stay) rather than an accidental repeat, and
  keep their emotional textures distinct (the Dim's is chosen solitude; the Moon's is a
  new self hidden in fog).
- Should the Corvenna family relatives be named at promotion, or stay unnamed walk-ons?
  Recommend unnamed — the son is the only named party the premise requires.
- Is there a small optional beat where the player may *lie* to the family (a gentler false
  ending)? Recommend against — the premise's whole point is the Fool delivers his answer,
  not a made-up one.
