# SQ-ASSIZE-03 — What the Ninefingers Sold

## Introduction

When Justice was unbound, the Assize's cells emptied — and among the three genuinely bad
actors who walked free was Corvin "Ninefingers" Rook, a smuggler and people-fence who once
moved stolen goods, and stolen people, across the suit-cultures against their will. He owes
nothing to anyone now, which is exactly the problem: the only man who knows where the lost
were sold is at large, unrepentant, and charging by the word. The Fool can press him —
carefully — for names, and turn one of them into a family that has grieved a living person
for decades. What comes back is unambiguously good news, wrapped around a man who profited
from the pain and will profit from ending it too, and won't pretend to be sorry about
either.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Post-`WS_JUSTICE_UNBOUND`, Rook (canon — `characters.md` §Recurring named
   NPCs) is found somewhere he can do business — the Assize steps, a Longroad inn, a
   Wheelhouse table. Charming, unbothered, free. Rumor holds he remembers exactly where
   every soul he fenced ended up.
2. **The transaction.** Rook will talk, but only for a price, and only a price — he offers
   no contrition and the Fool cannot shame one out of him (a haggle, Coins-flavored, that
   the Fool can drive down but never to nothing). He surrenders one thread: a coastal Cups
   family whose kin he sold inland, decades before the Stall froze his trial.
3. **The trace.** Following Rook's information — a pawned token, a ledger mark, Pip's nose —
   the Fool locates the sold one: alive, old now, having lived an entire life on the far
   side of that crime; or their descendants, whom the coastal family long ago gave up for
   dead.
4. **The complication.** Theme 3: the found life is not a tidy parcel to hand back. Perhaps
   the lost one has made a home they don't hunger to leave; perhaps the reunion is joyful
   but the searchers must reckon that the person they mourned is a stranger now. The Fool
   delivers the truth of it, not the ending anyone scripted.
5. **The ache and the laugh.** At its core the news is good — someone lost is found alive,
   and a Cups household that welcomes the Fool for it (Cups Renown, hospitality prized). The
   shadow the quest keeps: Rook profited a second time and walks off whistling, freed by the
   same mercy that freed everyone. The laugh: on parting, Rook tries to sell the Fool a "map
   to three more," transparently worthless; his shamelessness is the joke. The honest beat:
   the reunited family thanks the Fool, and Rook never once notices he wasn't thanked.
6. **Closing beat.** The coastal family's barks warm; the found kin's situation resolves per
   the truth delivered, not a happy default. Rook remains at large, unresolved on purpose,
   exactly as the freed-prisoner design intends. No `WS_*` flag is set.

## Open questions

- Decide the found kin's disposition: a living original (elderly, full life elsewhere) vs.
  descendants who never knew. Both fit Theme 3; the choice sets the reunion's exact ache and
  should be pinned before script status.
- Should Rook be a shared antagonist-broker reused by the other two freed-prisoner side
  quests (Gorrister Vale, the Widow Culpepper), or kept scoped to this one? Recommend scoped,
  to preserve each freed actor's distinct reveal.
- Whether the Fool may refuse to pay Rook at all and trace the thread unaided (longer, no
  coin cost). Recommend allowing it — it suits the Fool who won't reward a fence.
