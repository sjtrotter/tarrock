# SQ-VEIL-02 — What the Silence Costs

## Introduction

The Veil's archivists keep a vow of silence three centuries deep, and the whole cloister
is built around the withholding — nothing is asked twice, nothing is told at all. So when
one nun, Sister Aveline, breaks the vow to whisper the Fool a warning about "a shelf that
shouldn't be opened," the shock of it is entirely hers: she looks horrified at her own
voice the instant it leaves her, as if she has forgotten how loud a whisper can be in a
room that has heard none for three hundred years. The warning turns out to be correct — a
small, real hazard, nothing world-breaking — but the quest is not really about the shelf.
It is about a woman who must now sit with having *spoken at all*, in an order that
worships not speaking. A small, dry comedy about the one exception nobody ever thought to
test.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** Moving through the sealed stacks in the Veil's hush, the Fool is stopped
   by Sister Aveline (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs), who breaks three centuries of vow with a single hurried whisper: do not
   open the shelf in the north transept. Then her hands fly to her mouth and she goes
   rigid, appalled, as though the sound is still hanging in the air where anyone might see
   it.
2. **The warning proves out.** The Fool investigates the north transept and finds Aveline
   was right: the flagged shelf is genuinely unsafe — a wax-sealed run of overloaded
   stacks primed to come down, or a trapped reliquary drawer, or a book gone to spore.
   A modest hazard, cleared with care (light traversal / Pip's Seek, no boss), that would
   plainly have hurt someone had she said nothing. She was correct to speak, which somehow
   makes it worse for her.
3. **The complication.** With the danger past, Aveline is left holding the thing the
   cloister has no rite for: she spoke. She cannot un-speak. In an order where silence *is*
   the office (theme 2), one true and useful sentence has made her, in her own eyes, a
   different sort of nun than she took vows to be. The comedy is real — she keeps trying to
   apologise, in a whisper, and stopping herself, in a louder whisper.
4. **The ache and the laugh.** The Fool can reassure her, argue the vow's spirit (a
   warning that saves a life is surely what any real silence is *for*), or simply keep her
   company while she works it through. The honest beat under the farce: she confesses she
   is not sorry, and that not being sorry frightens her more than the speaking did. No one
   ever tested the exception, and now she is it.
5. **Closing beat.** Nothing world-scale turns on the outcome. Aveline's future barks
   shift at NPC level — a shade more willing to break hush for something that matters, or
   drawn back tighter into it, depending on how the Fool met her. The shelf stays cleared.
   The cloister, for now, keeps its silence around one small, permanent exception to it.
