# SQ-DIVIDE-01 — The Bridge That Almost Was

## Introduction

For three generations, a crew of amateur engineers led by old Cutwright Fenn has tried to
finish the Divide's unfinished bridge by hand — and has failed, every time, at the exact
plank the Stall left half-nailed. Lending a hand for one more attempt fails in precisely
the same way it always does, right on schedule, and Fenn takes it with a shrug worn
smooth by three generations of repetition — half genuine despair, half a running family
joke. It is a small, funny, aching rehearsal of the ending MQ06 owns, staged in timber
and rope by people who cannot know that the bridge was never waiting on carpentry.

## Beats

1. **The hook.** On the canyon rim, a rickety scaffold and a knot of tools: Cutwright Fenn
   (characters.md §Regional named NPCs) and his volunteer crew, three generations deep,
   rallying the Fool for "one more go" at the span the Betrothed's standstill froze
   mid-air.
2. **The work.** A genuine little construction-and-traversal beat — hauling timber,
   tensioning ropes, the Fool lending real hands over the gorge. It goes well, better than
   it ever has, right up to the one plank the Stall left half-driven.
3. **The wall.** That plank will not seat. Not by force, not by cleverness, not by any
   trick Fenn's father or grandfather ever tried. The bridge cannot be finished by hand,
   because the bridge was never waiting on carpentry — it is waiting on a choice two
   people will not make. The stone rehearses the story it stands inside.
4. **The honest beat / the laugh.** Fenn takes the failure with a shrug three generations
   have polished — half despair, half the oldest joke his family owns ("same plank, gods
   turn its card"). He keeps a tally of attempts carved into the scaffold; the Fool's is
   number four hundred and something.
5. **The ache (theme 1).** Fenn half-knows the truth: that some things cannot be built out
   of a frozen age, only *into* a moving one — and that the bridge will finish itself the
   day the world it is stuck in finally moves. He builds anyway, because the trying is how
   his family has kept faith that the age can move at all.
6. **Closing.** The crew packs up for the day, exactly as always, already planning
   tomorrow's identical attempt. [If WS_LOVERS_UNBOUND: the bridge completed overnight
   when the Betrothed at last chose, and Fenn stands at the finished span he spent his
   life failing to build — unsure whether to feel triumphant or robbed, his life's work
   done by someone else's wedding. In the end he decides a finished bridge is a finished
   bridge, walks across to the far town for the first time, and carves one last tally:
   "done."]

Rewards, if any: modest coins; a small rise in the local suit's Renown — the crew's craft
never finishing is rather the point, so nothing gated on completion.
