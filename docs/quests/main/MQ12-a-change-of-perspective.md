# MQ12 — A Change of Perspective

## Introduction

The player enters the Gallowwood braced for a fight, as every region so far has trained
them to be — and finds an invitation instead. There is no boss here and no combat at
all. The Hanged Man is the only Arcanum who is happy: three centuries hanging from the
World-Tree's bough gave his card precisely what it wanted, and he cannot be beaten, only
joined. This quest is a traversal ordeal through a forest that forgot which way gravity
was going — gravity-flipped glades, canopy bridges walked from beneath, the camera
honestly inverted in marked spaces — with a single let-go motion at its heart, a
deliberate rhyme with the leap of faith off the Cliff that opened the game. A canon
Gallowwood guide, Bracken Loft, leads the player partway and reads the flipped terrain
for them; hold onto him, because when the forest rights itself his life's expertise
becomes useless overnight, and MQ12 owns that mourning. The ordeal ends where the Fool
began it: hanging beside the Hanged Man, answering the one gentle question he ever asks.
He hands over Trump XII (Overturn) either way, delighted by both answers. If the player
reaches the Gallowwood after MQ13 (Death, "An Ending"), the key scenes carry
`[If CONFESSED: …]` variants — he is the one Arcanum who would find the news genuinely,
kindly funny.

## QUEST: A CHANGE OF PERSPECTIVE

### EXT. THE GALLOWWOOD: The Hanging Threshold — EVENING (STALLED)

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The forest starts wrong and never stops. The first trees stand ordinary enough — grey
boles, evening-gold light caught in a canopy that has held the same hour for three
hundred years — and then, a few steps in, a fallen leaf drifts *upward* past the Fool's
knee and settles, unbothered, on the underside of a branch. Further on, a whole glade
hangs the wrong way up: roots combing a sky, a stream running along a ceiling of moss,

### EXT. THE GALLOWWOOD: The Low Bough — EVENING (STALLED)

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The path pinches out over a shallow dell. A single low bough reaches across it, and on
the far side the trail resumes — but there is no way over except *under*: the only footing
is the underside of the bough itself, and the only way onto it is to hang.

Bracken Loft demonstrates without ceremony — reaches up, takes the branch, swings his
feet loose, and hangs there upside-down, entirely at home, pointing with his chin at the
handholds waiting along the bough's underside.

**[Tutorial prompt: leap and grab the low bough. The Fool hangs from it. Hold the grab;
the camera settles into the hang. This is the ordeal's whole motion in miniature.]**

**[Tutorial prompt: at the branch's midpoint, release. The Fool drops a short, safe
distance to the resumed trail below — the first *let go* the wood asks for.]**

The drop is nothing — a few feet of nothing — but the game has taught the player, for
eleven regions, that letting go of solid handholds ends badly. It does not, here.

**THE QUERENT** *(dry, warm)*
> You felt that, didn't you. That little argument your hands had with you before they
> opened. Hold onto it. You've done this once before — off a rather taller edge, first
> morning I knew you. Turns out it's the whole trick of this place, too.

**BRACKEN LOFT**
> There. Not so bad, letting go, when the wood's the one that's upside down and not you.
> Keep that feeling handy. The hollows ahead don't ask so nicely.

### EXT. THE GALLOWWOOD: The World-Tree — EVENING (STALLED)

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The wood opens onto the World-Tree, and the game's whole vocabulary of *up* comes apart at
once. It is vast past sense, roots and crown indistinguishable, and it does not hang the
wrong way so much as make the question meaningless — the eye can find no floor to measure
against. From its greatest bough, high and low and neither, hangs THE HANGED MAN: ankle
crossed over knee, arms loose, coat pooling toward a sky, a man three centuries into a good
hang and radiating a calm nothing else in the game has come close to earning.

At the foot of the tree — or the head of it — FENWICK SORREL, a threadbare pilgrim, is
camped amid notebooks fat with "almost-teachings," conducting an elaborate one-sided
conversation *upward* and getting, as ever, a serene near-nothing back.

**FENWICK SORREL** *(not to the Fool; upward, mid-oration)*
> —and so I put it to you again, master, that the suspension of the self IS the—
> [the Hanged Man makes a small, contented sound] —yes. YES. Write that down. That was
> definitely something. That was nearly the whole of it.

**[Tutorial prompt: approach the bough. Bracken Loft stops at the clearing's edge and
does not follow.]**

**BRACKEN LOFT** *(hanging back)*
> Far as I go. My rope's no good up there and neither am I. [he looks up at the hanging
> figure, and something crosses his face he doesn't name.] ...Go careful with him. He's
> the happiest thing I ever met, and I've never once been able to decide if that's a
> comfort or the saddest thing in this whole crooked wood.

The Hanged Man turns his head — unhurried, upside down, entirely unsurprised — and finds
the Fool with a look of plain, uncomplicated affection, as though he has been expecting
this particular visitor for a long and pleasant while.

**THE HANGED MAN** *(as they hang)*
> There. Feel your hands wanting to close? That's not fear, whatever they told you. That's
> just a body that never learned the difference between falling and flying. Neither did I,
> at your age. You unlearn it. You've all the time there is.

The circuit runs the whole vocabulary in sequence: hand-over-hand along inverted boughs;
a stretch of gravity-flipped glade where **the camera honestly inverts between marked
whorl-posts**; canopy bridges walked from beneath; a run of releases timed to swings, each
one a small let-go over a drop the game promises, again and again, is not the kind that
hurts. The Hanged Man peels away partway through to hang and watch, calling the occasional
unhurried encouragement across the gulf.

**THE HANGED MAN** *(across the glade, delighted)*
> Ha! You *let go* early there. On purpose. Oh, that's lovely — most people fight the whole
> way and arrive furious. You're arriving *curious*. That's much the better way to arrive
> at anything.

The circuit curves, by its own inverted logic, all the way round and back — and deposits
the Fool hanging once more from the great bough, upside-down and level with the Hanged
Man, exactly where it began.

**WENDEL**

### EXT. THE GALLOWWOOD: The Righting — EVENING (RIGHTING)

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

Over the next stretch, the whole wood turns over. It is not sudden — it is a long, visible,
almost tidal reshuffle, glade by glade, as three centuries of held-up gravity remembers
its business and lets everything down. Leaves that fell upward for three hundred years
reverse mid-drift and come down. The upside-down brook pours off its ceiling and finds a
bed. The canopy bridges swing round to hang the way bridges are meant to hang. Every path
the player learned reshuffles underfoot — the traversal is being re-taught in front of
them, honest-way-up.

**[Traversal reshuffle: the Gallowwood's marked-inversion spaces retire. The whorl-posts
now read as historical markers — "here the wood hung wrong." Feather-fall makes the new,
taller honest-way-up drops trivial, so the region stays a playground; it simply plays
right-way-up now.]**

Wendel walks the righting wood beside the Fool a little way, patient and delighted, a man
taking a first slow tour of a house he only ever saw from the ceiling.

Overhead — no: *underfoot* now, and unhappy about it — a canopy-dweller's cottage that was
built to the old gravity finds itself abruptly upside down. WICK ALDER stands in her own
doorway, which is now above her head, staring at a kettle that hangs from what has become
her ceiling, expression caught precisely between grief and a bark of laughter.

**WICK ALDER** *(to no one; to the whole righted wood)*
> ...Well. My front step's the roof now. [a long pause] Forty years I built that the
> right way up. The *old* right way up. [she doesn't finish; the arc is hers to walk, not
> the Fool's — SEED ONLY, resolved in SQ-GALLOWWOOD-02.]

### EXT. THE GALLOWWOOD: The Ordinary Forest — EVENING (RIGHTED)

happiness, quite, but a decision, which will do.

**BRACKEN LOFT**
> Right. Well. I'll keep guiding. Not the terrain — any fool can read this terrain now —

> three hundred years and never once thought to come down till a stranger with a dog asked

> ought to remember which way it used to hang. Might as well be the one man daft enough to
> have loved it.

He starts up the trail, telling the story to no one yet, practicing it — the Gallowwood's
first historian of how it used to hang, out of work and out of purpose and, against every
odd, not entirely out of joy.

> There's your ache for the day, little Excuse. He's right, of course. He usually is. The

> Onward. There's more cards, and every one of them costs somebody.

The wood ahead lies open, right-way-up, and lighter than it has been in three hundred
years. MQ12 ends here.

| Trigger | Flag(s) | Player-visible result |
|---|---|---|
| Quest completion (the ordeal's end; "Comfortable?" answered either way) | `WS_HANGEDMAN_UNBOUND` | The Gallowwood rights itself — gravity returns, the inverted glades and camera-inversion spaces retire, and the region's traversal reshuffles to honest-way-up (whorl-posts remain as historical markers). The Fool receives Trump XII (Overturn); its Past slot, Feather-fall, ends fall damage permanently. World-wide, penitent and anxious NPC barks soften — his peace spreads into the world's bark. Bracken Loft's guiding trade shifts from terrain-reading to living history; Wick Alder's home is now upside down (her arc opens in SQ-GALLOWWOOD-02). |

The answer to "Comfortable?" is a pure character/dialogue branch: both options are written,
both delight him, both unbind him, and **neither sets a distinct world-state flag** — there
is no mechanical fork, only which delighted line plays before the shared unbinding. Hence no
`branches` entry.

## Open questions

- **Bracken Loft's recurrence (carried from outline).** Should Bracken Loft persist as a
  Gallowwood ambient NPC after MQ12 — the region's living "historian of how it used to hang,"
  giving his self-appointed role a later payoff — or does his arc close on this quest's final
  beat? (SQ-GALLOWWOOD-02's Wick Alder is written as a *distinct* keeper of the old wood, so
  both can coexist; decision affects whether Loft gets recurring history-tour barks.)
- **Camera-inversion comfort/accessibility (carried from outline).** The "camera honestly
  inverted in marked spaces" needs technical confirmation of the opt-out path (stable camera
  + inverted-input tells, flagged the same by the whorl-posts). Script assumes both modes
  reach the same traversal; confirm at implementation before this stays `script`.
- **Tension flagged, not fixed — the Querent's wink placement.** The single permitted
  fourth-wall wink is spent in The Righting barks ("you're the hero of this story, and the
  kindest thing you did today was cut down a man who was perfectly happy"). It is the quest's
  thematic thesis, so it earns the slot — but confirm at review that no earlier Querent line
  (the arrival "staff up already," the traveler catch, the closing ache) reads as a *second*
  wink rather than warm narration.
- **Tension flagged, not fixed — does freeing Wendel read as loss to the player, per the
  tone bar?** The script leans hard into the ache (his wistful "I'll miss the view," the
  Querent's wink, Bracken Loft's whole arc) precisely because he is the hardest Arcanum to
  feel good about "defeating." Confirm at review that his consent (he hands the Trump gladly,
  he chose to come down) keeps it from tipping into the Fool having done something *wrong* —
  the intended note is bittersweet-right, not guilty.
