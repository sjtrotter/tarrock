# MQ00 — The Leap

## Introduction

The player begins here, with nothing to undo and nothing to remember — the Fool has just
been dealt into the world, and the Cliff is the first thing that world shows them. This
quest is the whole tutorial disguised as a morning: movement, Pip's Seek command, the
White Rose, first combat, and the first Waystation, all folded into one walk across a
lonely plateau at the world's broken edge. It ends with the game's real opening move —
the Fool steps off the Cliff on purpose — and the first sight of the Spread the player
will spend the rest of the game in. No world-state flags fire; completing MQ00 simply
opens the world by putting the Fool in it.

## QUEST: THE LEAP

### INT./EXT. VOID: Black Screen — BEFORE DAWN

CUT SCENE

Black. No image. A sound like cards being squared against a table, once, twice — and
then the unmistakable snap-slide of a single card being dealt.

**THE QUERENT** *(voice only)*
> One more card. There's always one more card.

A pause. Wind, faint, somewhere far off. Another card-snap, closer this time, almost
gentle.

**THE QUERENT**
> There you are. Good. Wake up, little Excuse — you're wanted.

END CUT SCENE

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The High Meadow — DAWN

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The screen brightens on a dead campfire, embers just gone grey, smoke thinning into a
sky the colour of a robin's egg. The Fool lies beside it. **[Player regains control:
tutorial prompt — move the left stick to sit up.]**

Pip is already awake, already sitting a few feet off, watching with the specific
patience of a dog who has been awake for some time and is not in any hurry about it.
He does not bark. He does not fuss. He simply waits, tail giving one polite thump against
the grass now that the Fool's eyes are open.

The White Rose lies loose in the Fool's hand, three petals closed and modest. The Bindle
— quarterstaff, bundle, and all the Fool owns — leans against a nearby stone.

**[Tutorial prompt: pick up the Bindle.]**

**[Tutorial prompt: move. The camera follows; a soft highlight marks the meadow's open
ground.]**

The meadow is high, wind-combed grass under a lightening sky, edged everywhere by a drop
the eye doesn't want to follow to its bottom. It is, as far as any horizon can tell,
completely empty of anyone else.

**THE QUERENT**
> Go on, then. Have a look at where you've landed. I'll wait — I've had three hundred
> years of practice at that particular skill.

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The Old Campsites — DAWN

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The Fool crosses the meadow toward a scatter of old fire-rings, each one long cold. Some
are barely rings at all any more — just a blackened circle in the grass, softened by
seasons of rain. Others still hold half a lean-to, a bootless boot, a bent tin cup, a
walking-stick standing upright in the turf as if its owner meant to come back for it.

None of the campsites are the same age. Some look decades gone. One, tucked under an
overhang, looks recent enough that the ash hasn't fully greyed.

**THE QUERENT**
> Every one of those belonged to somebody who woke up exactly where you did. Some of
> them stayed a night. Some of them stayed rather longer than that, by the look of it.
> All of them left the same way, in the end.

**[Tutorial prompt: interact with a fire-ring to inspect it. No dialogue triggers — this
is environmental only.]**

Pip trots ahead to a patch of disturbed earth near the largest campsite and begins to
dig with sudden, businesslike enthusiasm, tail a blur.

**[Tutorial prompt: call Pip's Seek command.]**

Pip backs out of the hole holding something small in his teeth and trots it proudly back
to the Fool: a whittled wooden dog, crude and palm-sized, one ear worn to a stub, the
carving gone smooth and dark with handling. Not fine work — somebody's own knife, somebody
without much skill at it, carving the one thing they had beside them because they wanted
a second copy to keep.

**THE QUERENT**
> Someone made that. For company, I'd guess, or luck, or because the nights up here are
> long and a knife needs something to do. Keep it. It's not much. It's yours now, all the
> same.

### CHOICE DIALOG — the wooden dog *(all questions may be exhausted)*

| The Fool | The Querent's response |
|---|---|
| Who carved this? | Somebody with a knife, a dog, and too much night to fill. That's all I know, and it's more than most keepsakes get. |
| Can dogs really walk with every Fool? | So they say. So Pip says, in his way — which is to say, not a word, and every answer you need. |
| I'll keep it safe. *(earnest)* | Good. That's rarer than you'd think, keeping a small thing safe. Most people save that for the large ones. |

[All versions pick up here:]

The wooden dog goes into the Bindle. Pip, satisfied with a job well done, trots on ahead
toward the meadow's far side.

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The Dead Tree — MORNING

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The ground rises to a modest knoll, and on it stands the only tree on the whole plateau
— bare, black-branched, bark peeling in long dry curls. Everything else on the Cliff is
green and windblown and entirely, aggressively alive. This one tree is not. It is the
single dead thing as far as the eye can see, and the meadow does not explain it, and
nothing grows in its shade.

**[Tutorial prompt: approach the dead tree. No combat, no item — a beat, held for a
few seconds before the Querent speaks.]**

**THE QUERENT** *(quieter than before)*
> That's the only thing up here that dies, you know. Everything else on this plateau
> just... carries on. Very restful, if you don't think about it too hard.
>
> This meadow sits outside the proper Reading, little Excuse — off the edge of the
> Spread, where the cards don't quite reach. Down there, further on than you can see
> yet, nothing has been allowed to end in three hundred years. Not one thing. That tree's
> the odd one out. Or perhaps it's the only honest one. I go back and forth.

A beat. Wind in the dead branches, dry as paper.

**THE QUERENT**
> Anyway. Nothing to be done about a tree. Onward.

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The Waystation Approach — MORNING

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The path narrows between two standing stones, and ahead — first sign of anything
built by hand — a modest wayside shrine sits half-swallowed in ivy: the Cliff's
Waystation.

Before the Fool can reach it, three figures rise from the long grass on either side of
the path: soldiers, human-shaped, blank ovals where faces should sit, each bearing a
faded tabard printed with a **2** and a suit mark — one Cups, one Swords, one Wands.
They advance without a word, because they have no mouths to say one with.

**[Tutorial prompt: light string — three quick strikes of the Bindle.]**

**[Tutorial prompt: dodge roll — a short burst of invincibility. Time it against an
incoming strike for Fool's Chance: a long beat of slow time (~1.5s, per combat.md) and
a free opening.]**

**THE QUERENT** *(mid-fight, easy)*
> Blanks. Twos — the lowest rung there is, so don't take it personally if this feels
> easy. Hit them, don't get hit back. It's not a more complicated plan than that for
> quite some time yet.

The three Blanks fall in turn — not dramatically, just definitively, staff-strikes
landing clean. Each one, on defeat, slumps like a puppet set down kindly — and the
card on its tabard peels free, tumbling upward on a wind that isn't there. Past the
ridge line, each drifting card settles onto a new blank-faced figure rising from the
grass, already walking off in a new direction entirely.

**[Tutorial prompt: none — this reassembly plays automatically, no player input.]**

**THE QUERENT**
> There they go. Don't fret over where — they'll turn up again, same as they always do.
> Nothing much ends around here just yet. You'll get used to the shape of that, one way
> or another.

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The First Waystation — MIDDAY

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The shrine up close: a stone basin, a scatter of old offerings gone to moss, a low arch
worn soft at the edges by hands the Fool will never meet. The White Rose, held near it,
stirs on its own — a petal unfurling with a small, private sound, like a breath let out.

**[Tutorial prompt: rest at the Waystation. The Rose regrows to full. The Pocket Spread
menu is introduced but empty — no Trump yet to slot.]**

**THE QUERENT**
> That's a Waystation. Rest here and the Rose comes back to you — regrows entirely, no
> waiting about. There'll be one of these in every region worth the name. Consider it
> the one promise I intend to keep without complication.

**THE QUERENT Random Lines** *(if the player rests again)*

> Still nothing to slot in that little spread of yours. That changes soon enough.

> Rest all you like. The meadow isn't going anywhere. Rather the point of the meadow,
> really.

> There. Better?

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The Cliff's Edge — AFTERNOON

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

Past the Waystation the meadow simply stops — a long, clean edge of turf and then sky,
the drop beneath it lost in haze. This is as far as the plateau goes. The Fool can walk
right up to where the grass ends and look out at absolutely nothing built, nothing
labeled, nothing to do here yet but ask questions of the only voice available.

**[Tutorial prompt: approach the cliff's edge to trigger dialogue.]**

### CHOICE DIALOG — questions at the edge *(all questions may be exhausted)*

| The Fool | The Querent's response |
|---|---|
| Who are you? | Someone who's waited a long while to have someone to talk to. That'll have to do for now. |
| What am I? | The Excuse, if you want the formal title — the one card that belongs to no position and may go anywhere at all. Handy, that. |
| What's wrong with the world? | The cards are stuck, little Excuse. Every last one of them, in place, since before your grandmother's grandmother. Go and turn them loose. |
| Why me? *(earnest)* | Because you're the one who got dealt. I don't make the rules of the Reading. I only get to ask the questions. |

**If the Fool asked "Who are you?":**

| The Fool | The Querent's response |
|---|---|
| Will you tell me eventually? | Perhaps. Ask me again somewhere further along, and further along after that. I'm not being coy — well. I am. But there's a reason. |

**If the Fool asked "What am I?":**

**THE FOOL**
> What am I, exactly?

**THE QUERENT**
> You're new, is what you are. No past to speak of, none needed — every wonder you're
> about to have is going to be a genuinely first one. I'd almost envy you, if envy were
> a thing I had time for.
>
> And — between us — you're also the one holding all of this together right now, aren't
> you. Every step, every choice. Funny how that works. Don't let it go to your head.

**THE FOOL**
> I'll try not to.

**If the Fool asked "What's wrong with the world?":**

| The Fool | The Querent's response |
|---|---|
| Stuck how? | Stuck like a wheel that won't turn, a sun that won't set, a bell that won't stop ringing the same hour. You'll see it plainly enough once you're down there. |
| Can I fix it? | "Fix" is a strong word for what's coming. "Finish" might serve better. Ask me again once you've actually met one of the stuck ones. |

[All versions pick up here:]

**THE QUERENT**
> That's rather a lot of honesty for one afternoon. I'll dole the rest out as we go —
> in fairness, so will the world. Go on. Have your look at the edge properly, this time.

### EXT. THE CLIFF: The Edge of the World — DUSK

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The light goes long and gold. Pip trots past the Fool's legs to stand right at the
lip of the drop, ears up, tail wagging with the frank enthusiasm of a dog who has done
this exact thing more times than anyone has bothered to count.

**[Tutorial prompt: approach the very edge. Pip will not wait long.]**

Pip jumps first — off the edge, into open air, tail still going, without a flicker of
hesitation.

CUT SCENE

**THE QUERENT**
> That's rather the whole method, you know. He never looks down first. Neither should
> you. It's not bravery, particularly. It's just what a Fool does when a Fool is done
> standing still.

**[Tutorial prompt: step off the Cliff.]**

The Fool steps out into nothing, and falls.

The fall becomes a flight becomes a long, held breath of a skydive over the whole
Spread laid out below like cards dealt face-up on a vast table: a wheel of regions
around one still, silver point at the very centre, where a single distant figure turns,
turns, turns, too far off to see clearly and too strange to look away from. Off to the
north, a tower stands snapped in half, rubble frozen mid-fall as if the world had
simply forgotten to let it finish. Off to the east, gold fields glare under a sun that
does not seem to be moving at all.

**THE QUERENT**
> There it is. The Spread, entire — the Axis at its heart, the Spire still coming down
> after three hundred years of trying, the Noonlands sitting under a sun that never once
> thought to set. It's beautiful and it's wrong and it has been both those things for
> longer than anyone still living can properly remember.
>
> Go and put that right, little Excuse. One card at a time.

The ground rushes up — and the Fool lands, with a considerable *whump* and no injury
whatsoever, in an enormous, improbably soft haywain parked at a crossroads, scattering
straw in every direction. Pip is already sitting in it, entirely unbothered, as though
this were the obvious and correct way to arrive anywhere.

A beat of birdsong and settling straw. Then, from somewhere close and delighted and
entirely too loud —

**FLICK** *(offscreen, first heard)*
> STEP RIGHT UP, step right up, don't mind the wagon, don't mind the straw, ladies and
> gentlemen and whatever *that* was that just fell out of the sky —

END CUT SCENE

The road ahead leads toward gaslit tents, striped canvas, and the unmistakable sound of
an audience that has been sitting in one place for a very long time. MQ00 ends here; MQ01
picks up at the Prestige's gate.

### BARKS — The Cliff *(idle exploration)*

> Take your time. I've waited three hundred years; a few more minutes won't kill me.
> Probably.
