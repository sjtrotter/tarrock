# MQ14 — The Perfect Measure

## Introduction

The player arrives at the Confluence, a delta city built on a single beautiful problem:
nothing here is ever allowed to *finish*. Bridges stand at exactly half-completion. A pot
of tea has been steeping three hundred years and is, by all accounts, nearly perfect. At
the delta's heart the Mixer pours forever between two colossal cups, blending a mixture
that can never be poured out, because pouring it out would mean it was done. This quest
teaches the player patience as a *skill* before it demands it as the only way to win: the
mini-challenges — a panicking apprentice's small blend, a crossing between the rivers' own
scald and frost, a mason's quiet plea — each drill the tempering the boss arena scales up.
The apprentice, Delphine Marchetti, also leads a side quest (SQ-CONFLUENCE-01) and is safe
to have met already; the mason, Elgin Thatch, is the quest's mourning NPC, planted early so
his return in the aftermath lands. MQ14 requires nothing and may be reached in any order; if
the player comes here after MQ13 (Death, "An Ending"), the Confluence already knows what a
visit from the Fool means, and its key scenes carry `[If CONFESSED: …]` variants.

## QUEST: THE PERFECT MEASURE

### EXT. THE CONFLUENCE: The Unfinished Bridge — DAY

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The road out of the last region ends at the foot of a bridge that has been almost finished
for three hundred years. Scaffolding still stands against it, the poles weathered smooth and
silver, the lashings gone to stone. The span reaches two-thirds of the way across a broad
brown channel and simply stops — a clean lip of dressed stone, a bucket of mortar beside it
gone hard as the rock it never joined. A plank walkway, added later and clearly meant to be
temporary, carries foot traffic the rest of the way.

Below and ahead, two rivers braid through a wide delta of mudbanks and reed-islands, running
toward a pair of cups too large to be architecture — vessels the size of cathedrals, tilted
toward one another at the delta's heart, a thread of something bright pouring endlessly
between them and never spilling a drop.

**[Tutorial prompt: cross the plank walkway. The stone span underfoot is warm; the plank is
not.]**

**THE QUERENT**
> The Confluence. Two rivers, one delta, and not a finished thing in the whole of it. Watch
> your step on the planks — the stone's the only part they trust, and the stone's the part
> that never got laid.

Pip pads out onto the dressed stone, sniffs the hard mortar bucket with grave interest, and
declines to cross the plank until the Fool goes first.

**THE QUERENT**
> Three hundred years of nearly. You'll find they're rather proud of it here. That's the
> thing to watch for — not the sadness. The pride.

### EXT. THE CONFLUENCE: Delphine's Mixing-House — DAY

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

An open-fronted mixing-house on a low wharf, its shelves ranked with copper and glass. Two
small cups stand on the counter — one scalding, steam rolling off it in ropes; one frosted
white, cold vapour spilling over its lip to the floor. Between them, mid-panic and clearly
losing, stands DELPHINE MARCHETTI, an apprentice with her sleeves shoved up and both hands
occupied, glancing between the two cups like a woman refereeing a fight she is also
somehow in.

Every time she tips the hot toward the cold it scalds; every time she tips the cold toward
the hot it freezes; and the blend she is after — the neutral, drinkable middle — keeps
slipping through the gap between the two disasters.

> to let it be forty-one.

### EXT. THE CONFLUENCE: The Twin-Cup Plaza — DAY

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

The plaza is the delta's heart, and it is not a plaza so much as the inside of the pour
itself. The two colossal cups tilt toward one another overhead, and between them, astride

THE MIXER does not stop pouring. She inclines her head, and her voice is calm as level

**THE MIXER**
> Ah. The unbalanced element. I wondered when you'd reach the middle. [she pours; scald into
> frost, frost into scald] You've upset something in every region you've passed through, and

**THE FOOL**
> Doesn't your arm get tired?

**THE MIXER**
> [If CONFESSED: I know what your presence means. I have measured it precisely — you finish
> things, and a finished thing is a poured-out thing, and a poured-out thing is *over*. I
> have spent three hundred years making certain nothing here is ever over. Tired? [the pour
> wavers a hair, recovers] I cannot afford to be tired. That is rather the point.]

> measure is perfect, and it will go on being perfect. You are the one thing in it that
> does not balance. So. Let us see if you can be blended in — or poured away.

END CUT SCENE

### CUT SCENE

The Mixer looks down at the place where the drop fell wrong. Around her, the office — the
serenity, the endless figure of the pour, three hundred years of a hand that never shook —
cracks. It does not shatter like glass or crumble like stone. It cracks *cleanly, once*,

**THE MIXER**
> It fell. [she watches it, uncomprehending, then dawning] It — a drop of it *fell*. I let a
> drop of it fall. Three hundred years and I never once — [her voice, for the first time,
> unsteady] — oh. *Oh.* I remember being tired. I had forgotten I was allowed.

**THE MIXER** *(barely above the water's sound)*

### EXT. THE CONFLUENCE: The Mixing-Houses — DAYS LATER

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

Along the wharves the mixing-houses have changed their whole rhythm. Where benches once held
blends forever nearly-done, mixers now brew things *through* — proper elixirs, poured out,
