# MQ08 — The Gentlest Hand

## Introduction

The player climbs into the Maw expecting the game's wilds region to test them with claws
and crags — and it does, with hostile beasts on every ledge and a whole hunting culture
that answers the wild the only way it knows: the cull. But the quest's real lesson waits
at the top of the climb, in a frozen tableau every hunter can point to and none will
approach: a woman who has held a lion's jaws open for three centuries, not by force but
by patience, and who asks the Fool for the one thing nobody has offered her since the
Stall — a turn. The road there teaches the grammar of the fight to come: a spooked
mountain cat that only worsens when struck, a wounded bear in a hunter's cull-trap that
can be freed with mercy or forced with violence. The encounter itself is a grapple-duel
with the lion — stamina wrestling and gentleness prompts where **attacking is the
mistake** — won by calm, not damage. Coz Yarrow, a border hide-hunter, walks much of this
road with the Fool and mourns its end: a Maw where beasts go neutral until provoked leaves
him with a spear and no reason to carry it. This quest requires nothing and may be played
in any order; if the Fool reaches the Maw after MQ13 (Death, "An Ending"), its key scenes
carry `[If CONFESSED: …]` variants — the woman on the ridge already senses larger endings
coming, and lays this one down gladly before the rest arrive.

### EXT. THE MAW: The Border Camps — DAY

The path out of the lowlands turns to bare limestone and keeps climbing. The Maw rises
ahead in grey vertical crags, scored and pitted like something that has been *chewed* —
which is, the hunters will tell you, exactly how it got the name. Woodsmoke and the sharp
tang of tanning-pits hang over a scatter of border camps: hide-frames strung with drying
skins, snares coiled on pegs, a whetstone singing somewhere out of sight. Everything here
is built for the taking of animals, and everything here is a little afraid of the animals
it takes.

### BARKS — the pilgrim shrines *(the climb to the ridge)*

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

came to *look*. It is lined with shrines — but they degrade as they climb, from the lower

content. The jaws close. Gently. On nothing. For the first time in three hundred years.

Calm holds.

### CUT SCENE — THE UNBINDING

The woman kneels in the settled dust and stares at the sleeping lion as though she has never
seen it before. Her empty hands are still open in her lap. And around her, at the edges — the
office begins to crack: the frozen grandeur of the tableau, the terrible patient posture, the
three hundred years of being *the one who does not tire* — all of it fissuring like old clay
off a face that was underneath the whole time.

> It's — held. It's *held*, and the hands are— *(she turns them over, slowly, staring)* —they're
> mine. They're mine. When did— how long since anything was—

She stops. Something surfaces through the crack — not injury, not the lion, but a name, coming
up the way feeling comes back into a limb that has been asleep a very long time: all at once,
and it stings.

**MAUD**
> ...*Maud.*
>
> Maud. I'm— *(the word catches; she has not said it in three centuries and it barely fits her
> mouth)* —I'm Maud. That's— gods. That's *me*. That was me the whole time, under the holding.

### EXT. THE MAW: The Ridge — CONTINUOUS

THIRD PERSON GAMEPLAY

Maud walks down off the ridge the ordinary way, on ordinary legs, and does not look back —
already, visibly, just a woman on a mountain, indistinguishable within a dozen steps from
anyone who ever climbed up to see her. The lion wakes at its own pace, stretches enormously,
regards the Fool with the frank incuriosity of a cat that has decided you are furniture, and
ambles off along the crag — in no hurry, threatening nothing, the single clearest picture in
the whole game of what freedom looks like for something that was never political about it.

The dozing goat gets up and trots after the lion. Neither of them finds this remarkable. Pip
watches them go with the specific longing of a dog who would very much like to make it three.
