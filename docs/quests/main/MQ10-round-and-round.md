# MQ10 — Round and Round

## Introduction

The player arrives in the Wheelhouse to a city split down the middle by luck itself:
gilded fortune on one side of the street, calamity on the other, neither having moved
in three hundred years. At the center of it all is a titanic stopped wheel, and the
quest's climax is the first thing to actually turn in this whole frozen casino — a
ring-arena that starts spinning the instant the fight begins.

## Beats

1. Arrival at the Wheelhouse: the lucky district gilded and absurd, the cursed district
   ruined and absurd in the opposite direction, one street apart.
2. The Fool meets Deuce Halloway (proposed), a croupier who walks the line between both
   districts daily and has stopped finding either of them funny.
3. Approach step: a rigged dice parlor in the lucky district, where "winning" too
   easily reveals exactly how wrong guaranteed luck feels from the inside.
4. Approach step: crossing the cursed district's hazard streets — falling signs,
   chronic bad luck made physical — to reach the access tunnels beneath the Wheel.
5. Approach step: Baroness Fettle (proposed), the wealthiest resident of the lucky
   district, meets the Fool at the tunnel mouth and tries to buy them off — her whole
   fortune depends on nothing here ever changing.
6. Reading the stasis: entry to the titanic stopped Wheel itself — a silent ring-arena,
   waiting, the first true quiet in the Wheelhouse's noise.
7. The encounter, exactly per `arcana.md` §X: the ring-arena begins turning the instant
   the fight starts — the first motion here in three centuries. Rotating "fortune
   weather" cycles buffs and calamities across arena segments, hitting the Fool and the
   Wheel's Blank crew alike; the objective is smashing the brake-shoes seizing the ring.
8. The fight's lesson lands mid-battle: fair and random are not enemies — read the
   weather, surf the luck, and the Wheel starts to feel like a dance partner.
9. Final brake-shoe destroyed: the great ring groans into true motion for the first time
   since the Stall.
10. Unbinding: the office cracks; their name returns; they hand the Fool Trump X
    personally, delighted, calling the Fool "the first unpredictable variable in three
    hundred years."
11. Aftermath: the Wheel turns; the Wheelhouse's luck districts begin cycling on a
    schedule; drop rates world-wide gain small, periodic fortune weather.
12. Mourning: Baroness Fettle watches her once-guaranteed fortune wobble for the first
    time, and finds an ordinary roll of the dice more frightening than any fight.
13. Closing beat: cursed-district residents pour into the street cheering as their luck
    visibly shifts for the first time in three hundred years — joy loud enough to drown
    out Fettle's dread, at least for one evening.

**Baroness Fettle** mourns the unbinding: her entire fortune was built on a rigged
wheel, and a fair one terrifies her far more than a cursed one ever did.
