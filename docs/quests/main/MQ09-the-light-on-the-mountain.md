# MQ09 — The Light on the Mountain

## Introduction

The player enters the Dim already knowing its one rumor: a lantern moves on the far
slopes every night, and nobody has ever caught it. There is no boss waiting in an arena
here — the mountain itself is the ordeal, cold and dark and built to be read, not
fought. Catching the light is not the victory. Being caught catching it is.

## Beats

1. Arrival at the Dim: a mountain of permanent dusk, cold wayfinding, hermit shacks
   scattered across star-blind valleys.
2. The Fool meets Widow Fenner (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) at a waypoint shack — she has fed and warmed
   lost travelers for years and describes the light seen nightly on the far ridges,
   never caught.
3. Approach step: tracking a trail of cold embers and half-buried footprints across a
   scree slope to the light's first sighting — the chase's core reading-the-land skill.
4. Approach step: crossing a star-blind valley by landmark and by Pip's nose rather than
   by sight, since there is nothing overhead to steer by — a no-combat navigation puzzle.
5. Approach step: meeting lost travelers who have built cairns marking false leads, one
   of whom insists, cheerfully wrong, that the light is a ghost.
6. The chase tightens: the light appears closer each night, and slips away each time
   moments before the catch — establishing that the region itself is the boss.
7. Reading the stasis: the Fool understands the pattern at last — the light is always
   on the next ridge, and has been for three hundred years, because nobody had ever
   followed far enough to be worth catching.
8. Final approach: using the terrain knowledge earned across the chase, the Fool
   outpaces the pattern for the first time and closes the distance.
9. The encounter, exactly per `arcana.md` §IX: he's sitting beside the lantern with two
   cups already poured, because being caught was the point — a lantern is for being
   followed, and nobody had followed in three hundred years.
10. He asks the Fool one question over tea — proposed: "What have you been looking for,
    all this way?" — the answer is filed away, to be barked back by NPCs much later.
11. Unbinding: he hands over the lantern like it weighs a mountain, because it does; his
    name returns to him in the same motion.
12. Aftermath: stars return over the Dim; lost-traveler NPCs come down from the hills; a
    sidequest chain unlocks across four regions.
13. Mourning: Widow Fenner's shack empties as the last of her regulars finally head home.
14. Closing beat: she keeps the kettle on anyway, "in case anyone's still finding their
    way" — half habit, half hope, and the quest's one small, deliberate laugh.

## Key NPCs

- **The Hermit** (freed name Ellery, `characters.md` §IX) — withdrew to think and never
  stopped; must be caught, not beaten.
- **Widow Fenner** (canon, `characters.md` §Regional named NPCs) — a
  waypoint-shack keeper for the Dim's lost travelers; this quest's mourning NPC.

## Mourning

**Widow Fenner** mourns the unbinding: her whole purpose was tending travelers who
couldn't find their way, and the Dim, found at last, needs a great deal less tending.

## [If CONFESSED] variants

  answer will eventually cost, and listens harder for it.
- Widow Fenner's mourning bark becomes about time itself moving now, not merely about

  spoken with equal parts awe and dread.
